# Manual of Style — MDI General Audience (The Sweet Spot)
*For: The Weaponization of Compassion and the Material Dignity Series*
*Reader Profile: General audience, 8th to 10th-grade reading level*

---

## Register

Industrial precision combined with human rhythm. The prose must achieve the "Architectural Density Sweet Spot," condensing source logic into the minimum necessary lexical volume without becoming robotic. Logic must flow entirely through syntax and verb choice, never through artificial transition words. 

---

## Core Structural Mechanics

### 1. The Anti-Slop Index (Zero Mechanical Transitions)
Cut on sight: *furthermore, moreover, additionally, as a result, consequently, therefore, thus, hence, subsequently, finally, in summary*. 
Logic carries transitions; words marking them announce their absence. Sentences must link through active consequence. 
*   **Failure (Robotic):** They tapped the pole. As a result, they had power.
*   **Standard (Flowing):** Tapping the city light pole provided the necessary power to run the underground grid.

### 2. Sentence Rhythm (The Long/Long/Short Hammer)
Eradicate staccato fragmentation. Sentences may expand to 35 or 40 words when exploring complex mechanical interactions, provided they are anchored by strong verbs. This tension must be released deliberately. Follow complex structural explanations with a brutal, five-to-ten word declarative hammer. Vary sentence length continuously within each paragraph.

### 3. Opener Rotation
Stop opening every sentence with a subject noun. Rotate sentence openings using time markers (*After the sweep,*), conditions (*If the city cuts the power,*), participles (*Splicing live high-voltage wiring,*), and prepositional phrases (*Beneath the concrete bridge,*). No more than half the sentences in a paragraph may open directly with the subject.

### 4. Syntactic Bridging
Establish structural flow through coordinate conjunctions (*and, but, so, yet*) and subordinate conjunctions (*because, while, although, since*). Combine related ideas into singular, flowing causal chains rather than isolating every step into its own period.

---

## Content and Tone Constraints

### 5. The Affirmative Mandate (Zero Binary Foils)
Absolute prohibition on "Not X but Y" structures. The word "not" is functionally banned as a rhetorical contrast device. State exactly what a concept, action, or outcome IS. Logic must stand on its own mechanical merit.

### 6. Metaphor-Mapping Requirements
Replace abstract academic or policy terminology with intuitive urban, mechanical, or industrial metaphors. 
*   "Institutional capture" translates to a "budget engine" or "revenue machine."
*   "Ontological security" translates to a "secure physical foundation."
*   "Metabolic stabilization" translates to "biological recovery."

### 7. Lexical Neutralization (The Sentinel Standard)
*   **No Em-Dashes**: Establish structural flow through syntax.
*   **No Parenthetical Phrases**: State all information directly within the primary sentence structure.
*   **No Weak Qualifiers**: Reject word-padding: *actually, merely, simply, fundamentally, explicitly, essentially, absolutely, perfectly, however*.
*   **No Academic Clichés**: Reject filler: *utilize, framework, paradigm, nuanced, mitigate, synergy, impactful*.
*   **No Simplistic Filler**: Reject instructional bridging: *for example, let us explore, it is important to note*.
*   **No Intensity Crutches**: Reject words that artificially inflate gravity: *exact, exactly, specific, specifically, direct, directly, precise, precisely, massive, massively, entire, entirely, complete, completely, highly, severe, severely, pure, purely, strict, strictly, constant, constantly*. Nouns and verbs must carry the weight unassisted.

### 8. Topic Sentence Primacy
The first sentence of every paragraph states the paragraph's central claim directly. Evidence and elaboration naturally flow from that initial declaration.

---

## Application Notes
Write so that the mechanical truth of the situation arrives on the first pass, carried by a cadence that feels natural, authoritative, and human. The reader must be able to "see" the gears turning without being jarred by robotic, staccato syntax.
