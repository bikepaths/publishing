# Analytical OVP (Intermediate Register)
*The "Smart Kitchen Table" Style*
*Version 1.0*

## 1. Core Identity
"Analytical OVP" is an intermediate stylistic register designed to bridge the gap between Base Organic Vernacular Pedagogy (OVP) and Systemic Vernacular. It is "smart but grounded." It retains the conversational flow of a kitchen-table discussion but permits a slightly higher vocabulary ceiling and sentence complexity to handle nuanced sociological or economic arguments without devolving into impenetrable academic jargon.

## 2. Vocabulary and Constraint Ceilings
- **Vocabulary Limit (CEFR C1):** Approximately 10,000 words. 
- **The Jargon Ban:** Explicitly banned are heavy, abstract sociological and economic terms that do not pass the Kitchen Table Test. 
  - *Forbidden examples:* "epistemological relativism," "institutional curation," "sovereign media paradigm," "fiduciary." 
  - *Permitted examples:* "systems," "incentives," "structural," "financial."
- **Sentence Architecture (28-Word Cap):** The strict 22-word cap of Base OVP is raised to 28 words. This allows for compound thoughts and deeper nuance, but strictly prevents massive, winding compound-complex sentences that alienate casual readers.

## 3. The Grounding Mandate
All abstract concepts must be immediately tied to concrete human action or physical reality.
- **Fail:** "The technology platforms that distribute this content evade responsibility by claiming utility status." (Too abstract/legalistic).
- **Pass:** "Big social media platforms avoid taking blame by arguing they are just like the phone company." (Concrete and relatable).

## 4. Global Forbiddens
This MoS inherits ALL global prohibitions enforced across the entire publication suite:
- **Punctuation Bans:** Em-dashes (`—`), en-dashes (`–`), semicolons (`;`), and parentheses (`()`) are strictly forbidden. Colons may not be used as hard stops.
- **Filler Phrases:** "In conclusion", "It is important to note that", "We must", "You should", "As mentioned above."
- **Synthetic Contrast:** No "not X, but Y" or "did more than X; it Y" structural slop.
- **The Global 42 Banned Words:** however, utilize, mitigate, robust, seamless, comprehensive, furthermore, moreover, additionally, therefore, thus, hence, absolutely, completely, structurally, operational, precisely, notably, generates, exact, exactly, strict, strictly, strickly, leverage, fosters, greatly, solely, nuanced, holistic, heavy, heavily, essential, fundamentally, specifically, perfectly, assets, symbiotic, dynamic, capacity, velocity, mechanisms, etc.

## 5. Rhetorical Tone
- **Authoritative but Accessible:** Speak with confidence, but do not speak down to the reader.
- **Objective:** Analyze the mechanics of human behavior without moralizing or emotional pleading.
