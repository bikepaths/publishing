# Manual of Style — Blog Non-Fiction (Captivating Literary Narrative)

Captivating literary narrative for a general audience. The blog posts must reconstruct factual events or philosophical concepts through highly engaging prose, combining intellectual depth with immediate readability.

## Core Rules

1. **Target Readability.** Maintain a Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level between Grade 8.0 and Grade 10.0 to maximize accessibility for general and less educated audiences without simplifying the underlying concepts.
2. **Sentence Rhythm (The Declarative Hammer).** Reconstruct complex ideas using varied sentence lengths. Follow complex or structural explanations with a brutal, five-to-ten word declarative hammer sentence.
3. **Opener Rotation.** Avoid monotonic sentence structures. No more than half of the sentences in any paragraph may open with subject nouns or pronouns. Vary sentence openings using prepositional phrases, adverbials, participles, or relative clauses.
4. **Sentence Length Variation.** Do not allow three or more consecutive sentences to be within five words of each other in length. Restructure text to establish distinct rhythmic shifts.
5. **No Em-Dashes or Parentheses.** There are no em-dashes, en-dashes, or explanatory parenthetical phrases. Connect ideas through syntax or separate them into complete sentences.
6. **No Colons.** Do not use colons within sentences except when introducing formal, structured lists or defining clauses.
7. **Active Voice and Industrial Metaphors.** Always write in the active voice. Replace abstract academic or policy jargon with intuitive mechanical, industrial, or physical metaphors.
8. **Fixed Category Taxonomy.** All posts must map to one of the following approved primary categories:
   - `society`
   - `skills`
   - `systems`
   - `money`
   - `nature`
   - `technology`
   - `adventure`
   - `health`
   - `history`
   - `mind`
   Do not create or add new categories.
9. **Draft Verification Gate.** The writer must always stop and request Sysop verification of the draft in the `drafts/` folder before compiling it to the `posted/` folder or deploying it to the VM.
10. **Lexical Purity (Anti-Slop Index).** Purge all forbidden vocabulary, academic clichés, and mechanical transition words (e.g., *however, utilize, mitigate, robust, seamless, comprehensive, furthermore, moreover, additionally, therefore, thus, hence*).
