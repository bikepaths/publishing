# JSDH Submission Plan: The Moral Physics of Survival

## Overview

* **Target Journal**: *Journal of Social Distress and Homelessness*
* **Submission Type**: Theoretical / Conceptual Article
* **Key Focus**: Booklet 3 (*The Moral Physics of Survival*)

## Manuscript Outline

### 1. Introduction
Modern municipal policy responses to chronic homelessness remain constrained by sociological paradigms and administrative case management. By analyzing the systemic failures of standard housing programs, this section introduces the necessity of physical, infrastructure-first interventions.

### 2. The Biological Floor
Street exposure, chronic sleep deprivation, and environmental trauma destroy prefrontal cortex function, dismantling executive capacity. Under these conditions, the standard economic and political assumption of rational choice ceases to operate.

### 3. Secular Natural Law
To establish a rigorous ethical baseline, this section outlines a secular framework derived from biological survival needs and human nature. Grounding dignity in biological requirements replaces subjective compassion with objective material standards.

### 4. Reconstructing the Social Contract
Modern political philosophy assumes citizens possess intact cognitive agency. By exposing this hidden assumption, the paper demonstrates that civil society must guarantee the physical prerequisites of biological agency before expecting social participation.

### 5. Ethical Legitimacy of Compelled Care
When cognitive agency is severely compromised, removing individuals from self-destructive street precarity becomes a moral necessity. Placing vulnerable populations within protective clinical-housing environments satisfies the civic hierarchy of obligation.

### 6. Infrastructure as Public Utility
Standardized, self-sustaining sanitation pods and Vertically Integrated Stabilization Towers convert survival services into a permanent public utility. Deploying physical engineering solutions stabilizes vulnerable populations and reduces municipal emergency healthcare expenditures.

### 7. Conclusion
Urban design remains the most direct mechanism for arresting bodily decay. Prioritizing biological stabilization over immediate programmatic compliance establishes a solid foundation for subsequent psychological and economic recovery.
