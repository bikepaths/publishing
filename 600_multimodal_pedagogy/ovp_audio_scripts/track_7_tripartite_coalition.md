# OVP Audio Track 7: The Tripartite Coalition

**Target Audience**: Policy makers, capital allocators, and civic leaders.
**Body-First Focus**: The systemic friction of misaligned incentives (clinical vs. legal vs. financial).
**Core Message**: "Homelessness is a highly profitable industry for the systems managing it, but a catastrophic loss for the municipality."
**Systemic Concept Translation**: Aligning managed care, private capital, and municipal zoning to bypass NIMBYism and accelerate stabilization infrastructure.

---

## Episode 1 Dialogue Draft: Follow the Friction

**[Ambient Sound: The rhythmic hum of server racks and fluorescent lights. Clinical, cold, precise.]**

**NARRATOR:**
Everyone is pointing fingers. The hospitals blame the police. The police blame the courts. The courts blame the shelters. And meanwhile, the bodies stay on the concrete. 

The problem isn't a lack of money. The problem is that the money is legally prohibited from actually solving the crisis. 

**[Beat. One second of silence.]**

**NARRATOR:**
We built a machine with three engines pulling in opposite directions. You have clinical money—Medicaid, managed care—that pays to treat the symptoms of the street, but by law, it cannot buy the brick and mortar to get people off the street. You have municipal money tied up in zoning wars, fighting neighborhoods that refuse to build. And you have private capital sitting on the sidelines because there is no predictable return on investment. 

We call the solution the Tripartite Coalition. We stop asking the city to do everything.

We use clinical revenue to guarantee the lease. We use private capital to build the modular infrastructure. And we use state-level preemption laws to bypass local zoning boards. When you lock those three gears together, the machine stops pulling itself apart. 

You don't need another task force. You need a legally binding coalition that aligns the financial incentives. Stop fighting the politics. Engineer the economics.
