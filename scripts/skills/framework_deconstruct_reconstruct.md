# Skill — Fact-Based Deconstruction and Reconstruction

This skill governs the systematic rewriting of chapter drafts to eliminate all mechanical stiffness, structural deviations, and forbidden qualifiers. It separates content from form through two sequential phases.

## Phase 1: Fact Deconstruction

The agent must read the source chapter draft and extract a clean list of atomic, objective facts. 

### Extraction Rules

- Extract only factual claims verified by the draft text.
- Formulate each fact as a single, independent statement.
- Remove all transitions, adjectives, analogies, and rhetorical framing.
- Use plain, dry, and objective language.
- Do not introduce external facts or interpretations.
- Group the facts by their original logical sequence.

## Phase 2: Prose Reconstruction

Using only the extracted facts list from Phase 1, the agent must rebuild the chapter from scratch.

### Writing Rules

1. **Sequential Flow**: Every sentence must grow logically from the conclusion of the previous one. Ensure causality is established through active verb phrasing.
2. **Pacing and Scale**: Maintain an unhurried, deliberate rhythm. Use long paragraphs to build sustained thoughts.
3. **Purity of Prose**: Do not use headers, bullet points, numbered lists, or bold inline labels.
4. **Punctuation Limits**: Do not use colons, em-dashes, or parenthetical phrases. Connect ideas using natural sentence movement or complete sentences.
5. **Forbidden Vocabulary**: Eliminate all prohibited qualifiers, academic clichés, and mechanical transitions.
6. **Sentence Variation**: Vary sentence openings. Ensure no more than forty percent of sentences within a paragraph open directly with the subject noun. Vary sentence lengths so that no three consecutive sentences fall within five words of each other.
