## Compiled Facts from Original Post
- Bokator is an ancient Khmer martial art carved in the Angkor temples for over ten centuries.
- Traditional Khmer martial arts focus on self-control, respect, humility, and ethical behavior rather than combat competition.
- Physical disciplines include punches, kicks, elbows, knees, clinching, grappling, and ground fighting.
- Practitioners train with traditional weapons including staffs, swords, and knives.
- Progression is structured through a belt ranking system requiring technical skills and character growth.
- Surviving masters preserved the art during the Khmer Rouge regime.
- Training builds neural pathways supporting focus, persistence, self-esteem, and prosocial habits.

## Additional Research Facts
- UNESCO inscribed Bokator on the Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity in 2022.
- The name Bokator translates to pounding a lion, originating from a legendary duel between a warrior and a lion.
- The system contains animal styles including monkey, elephant, and bird forms.
- Practitioners wear traditional krama scarfs and sangvar cords during training and ceremonies.
- The ultimate rank in Bokator is the gold krama, following the black krama.
