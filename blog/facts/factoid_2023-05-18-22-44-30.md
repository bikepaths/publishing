## Compiled Facts from Original Post
- Frédéric Bastiat argued that growing bureaucracies and red tape stifle economic progress and infringe on liberty.
- Henry Hazlitt asserted that government intervention distorts market mechanisms and misallocates resources.
- Hilaire Belloc warned that unchecked capitalism concentrates wealth and power, which leads to social unrest and a servile state.
- For income inequality, Bastiat favored removing barriers to entrepreneurship, and Hazlitt proposed improving education and skills training rather than redistributing wealth.
- Belloc emphasized worker rights, fair wages, and a safety net to protect the marginalized.
- On trade, Bastiat advocated for free trade, Hazlitt emphasized national security, and Belloc cautioned against foreign dependency to ensure stability.

## Additional Research Facts
- Distributism, championed by Belloc, posits that a society is healthiest when property ownership is decentralized and held by many households rather than the state or large corporations.
- Laissez-faire economics, championed by Hazlitt and Bastiat, argues that market competition naturally organizes society more efficiently than any centralized agency.
- The debate highlights the tension in modern governance between market efficiency, national security, and social cohesion.
