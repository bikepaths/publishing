## Additional Research Facts
- In 1894, Annie Londonderry left Boston on a global expedition, becoming the first woman to bicycle around the world.
- Annie Londonderry funded her journey by carrying advertisements on her bicycle, starting with a $100 sponsorship from the Londonderry Lithia Spring Water Company.
- During her trip, Annie Londonderry switched from a heavy 42-pound women's Columbia drop-frame bicycle to a lighter 21-pound men's Sterling bicycle.
- To improve her cycling efficiency and comfort on the road, Annie Londonderry rejected Victorian skirts in favor of men's riding bloomers.
