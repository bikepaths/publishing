## Additional Research Facts
- Hilaire Belloc was a co-founder of Distributism, which advocates for the widespread ownership of private property rather than concentration in the hands of the state or a few capitalists.
- Belloc argued that welfare policies like minimum wage and unemployment benefits codify worker dependency on the state.
- Henry Hazlitt was an Austrian School economist who defended free-market capitalism, private property, and free trade.
- While Hazlitt viewed free markets as the solution to poverty, Belloc believed capitalism naturally degenerates into servitude without distributed ownership.
