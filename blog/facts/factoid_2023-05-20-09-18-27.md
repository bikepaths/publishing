## Compiled Facts from Original Post
- Key barriers to everyday utility cycling include inadequate bike lanes, safety concerns in mixed motor traffic, long commuting distances, and inclement weather.
- Car-centric development in American cities historically prioritized wide roads and sprawling zoning, making infrastructure retrofitting difficult.
- Solutions require systemic changes like building dedicated cycling lanes, adding workplace shower facilities, and increasing driver safety education.

## Additional Research Facts
- The Copenhagenize Index, which ranks global cities on utility cycling metrics, consistently places European capitals like Copenhagen and Amsterdam at the top.
- Post-World War II zoning laws in the United States promoted low-density suburban sprawl, which locks in long commute distances incompatible with daily cycling.
- Protected bike lanes, or cycle tracks physically separated from car traffic, reduce cyclist injury rates by up to ninety percent compared to shared roads.
