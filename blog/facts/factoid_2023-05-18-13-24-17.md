## Compiled Facts from Original Post
- Safety concerns from traffic and a lack of cycling infrastructure discourage people from riding.
- Physical disabilities and health limitations make comfortable cycling challenging or impossible.
- Anxiety and a lack of cycling experience make individuals hesitant to ride.
- Long travel distances and steep, demanding terrains make cycling impractical for daily transport.
- Extreme weather conditions like heavy rain, snow, and extreme heat deter cyclists.
- Time constraints on busy schedules make cycling less viable than driving or public transit.
- Access barriers such as high bicycle costs and a lack of bike lanes make cycling less feasible.

## Additional Research Facts
- In U.S. surveys, approximately 54% to 75% of potential cyclists cite safety fears and vehicle collisions as primary deterrents.
- A 13-year study across 12 major U.S. cities showed that streets with protected bike lanes had 44% fewer fatalities and 50% fewer serious injuries for all road users.
- Traffic-calming effects of protected bike lanes improve safety by encouraging lower and more uniform vehicle speeds.
- Disparities in infrastructure investment lead to higher crash rates and sidewalk riding in low-income neighborhoods.
- Black cyclists in the United States are disproportionately represented in traffic-related fatalities.
