1. SERIES DESCRIPTION (Plain Text for KDP Series Listing)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
The Material Dignity Infrastructure series provides a falsifiable, mechanical blueprint to solve unsheltered chronic homelessness. Written by Charles J. DiBella, this series integrates thirty years of street-level observation with stress neurobiology, urban economics, and commercial real estate to propose a permanent public utility model.

Volume 1, Building Material Dignity, outlines the physical architecture of Vertically Integrated Stabilization Towers—engineered environments designed for neurobiological decompression and safety behind locked, acoustically isolated doors.

Volume 2, The Architecture of Survival, exposes the misaligned financial incentives of the legacy homelessness services industry, replacing a twenty-four billion dollar municipal failure with a verified four-stage industrial recovery pipeline.

Volume 3, The Moral Physics of Survival, constructs the philosophical and ethical foundation for this system, demonstrating how chronic street exposure destroys human cognitive agency and establishing the moral legitimacy of compelled clinical intervention.

This series is not an appeal to abstract compassion. It is a rigorous manual for civic leaders, economists, and urban developers seeking to replace municipal collapse with structural stability.

4. BOOK 3: THE MORAL PHYSICS OF SURVIVAL
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

=== HTML VERSION (Copy and paste into Amazon KDP Description Form) ===
<p><strong>Universal dignity rests on human nature rather than performance or functional capacity. Below the biological floor, choice and consent cease to operate.</strong></p>

<p>For decades, political philosophies and municipal programs have assumed that unsheltered individuals possess intact cognitive agency. Thirty years of street-level observation and neurobiological research prove otherwise. <em>The Moral Physics of Survival</em> provides the ethical and empirical framework to justify direct, systemic intervention.</p>

<p>Charles J. DiBella outlines the moral necessity of the Material Dignity Infrastructure (MDI). When street exposure, sleep deprivation, and chronic trauma destroy prefrontal cortex function, biological recovery must precede any housing choice or behavioral contract. We are morally obligated to protect the vulnerable through compelled clinical intervention and public utility design. This is not an abstract theory; it is an empirical science mapping the causal mechanisms of municipal collapse.</p>

<p>In this final volume of the series, you will discover:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>The Biological Floor</strong>: Why exposure to the street dismantles the brain's executive function and invalidates the standard assumption of rational choice.</li>
<li><strong>Natural Law Without God</strong>: How to construct a secular, empirical ethical framework based on biological survival needs and human nature.</li>
<li><strong>The Social Contract's Hidden Assumption</strong>: Why modern political philosophy fails to account for the physical requirements of biological agency.</li>

<li><strong>The Legitimacy of Compelled Care</strong>: The ethical justification for removing individuals from self-destructive street exposure and placing them in clinical-housing environments.</li>
</ul>

<p><em>The Moral Physics of Survival</em> is the philosophical capstone of the Material Dignity Infrastructure series. It bridges the gap between physical engineering, public policy, and moral law. The choice is not between compassion and coercion, but between survival and systemic abandonment.</p>
