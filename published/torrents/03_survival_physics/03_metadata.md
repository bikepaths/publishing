SYNOPSIS:
This volume establishes philosophical foundations for material dignity infrastructure. Drawing on thirty years of direct observation at street level, it exposes how well-meaning institutions capture state funding and align internal incentives toward self-preservation, ignoring the basic biological floor of the unhoused population. It maps the physiology of shelter, the thermodynamics of exposure, the neurobiology of trauma, and the legitimacy of compelled care.
