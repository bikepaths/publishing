# Chapter 10 Atomic Facts

## Section 1: Anosognosia and Consent Constraints
- Anosognosia is a neurological incapacity to perceive one's own psychiatric condition, produced by frontal lobe damage.
- Under anosognosia, individuals cannot process the fact that they are in danger.
- The current system treats the absence of voluntary consent as a binding constraint for individuals with anosognosia.

## Section 2: CARE Court Scope and Structure
- California's CARE Court was established under the Community Assistance, Recovery, and Empowerment Act of 2023.
- CARE Court became operational across all fifty-eight California counties by 2024.
- The law targets adults with schizophrenia spectrum disorders, other psychotic conditions, and bipolar disorder with psychotic features, which was added in early 2026.
- Petitions can be filed by families, roommates, first responders, healthcare providers, or the MDI Stewardship Authority.
- The court establishes a twelve to twenty-four month CARE Agreement.
- A CARE Agreement is a court-monitored community treatment plan specifying housing, medication compliance, and wraparound clinical services.
- Individuals in the CARE Court system remain in the community under low-restriction supervision.
- The MDI model assumes that only fifteen to twenty-five percent of the Pipeline C cohort will successfully traverse court-mandated pathways.


## Section 3: HMIS Integration with CARE Petitions
- The MDI Stewardship Authority's by-name HMIS registry provides behavioral records, recorded incidents of psychosis, dangerous behavior, or service refusals for CARE Court petitions.
- The MDI Stewardship Authority is an authorized petitioning entity.
- The registry triggers legal action for individuals without involved family representation.

## Section 4: Laura's Law and Assisted Outpatient Treatment
- Laura's Law, also known as Assisted Outpatient Treatment, operates as a civil court order mandating medication compliance and outpatient clinical engagement.
- AOT applies to individuals with a history of treatment non-compliance and at least one prior psychiatric hospitalization, incarceration, or documented act or threat of violence.
- Non-compliance triggers civil court review and escalation rather than criminal charges.
- Petitions use evidence from the HMIS registry's history of hospitalizations and system contacts.
- Successful AOT completion often serves as a prerequisite for transitioning out of specialized MDI housing programs.


## Section 5: Lanterman-Petris-Short Conservatorship
- Lanterman-Petris-Short, also known as LPS, Conservatorship applies to individuals deemed gravely disabled and unable to provide for their own food, clothing, or shelter due to mental health or substance use conditions.
- Senate Bill forty-three of 2023 expanded LPS qualifying conditions to include severe substance use disorders.
- A court-appointed conservator makes medical and housing decisions on behalf of the individual.
- Conservatorship allows the conservator to accept an Adaptive Long-Term Medical Unit placement on the individual's behalf.
- Ongoing conservatorship review can restore legal autonomy as the individual's psychiatric symptoms improve.

## Section 6: Legal Jurisdictional Bypass
- The MDI modular unit must be legally classified as deployable emergency medical equipment under a Metropolitan Stabilization Node designation in the state health code.
- The Stewardship Authority executes master leases with the Army Corps of Engineers or Department of Defense on federal land to bypass municipal zoning blocks.
