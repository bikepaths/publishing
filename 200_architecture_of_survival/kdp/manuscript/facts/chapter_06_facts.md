# Chapter 6 Atomic Facts

## Section 1: Demographics and Pipelines
- The 2025 Los Angeles Homeless Services Authority point-in-time count recorded approximately seventy-five thousand unhoused individuals in Greater Los Angeles County.
- Roughly forty-seven thousand of these individuals slept outside on any given night.
- Ground-level counting methods miss the hidden riparian population.
- The unhoused population consists of four distinct groups requiring different interventions.
- The taxonomy functions as a state machine where individuals transition between pipelines as stability fluctuates.


## Section 2: Pipeline A for Temporary Economic Displacement
- Pipeline A contains approximately twelve thousand two hundred individuals experiencing temporary economic displacement.
- These individuals lost housing due to financial shock, such as medical emergencies, job loss, evictions following domestic disputes, or discharge from hospitals or prisons without transitional placements.
- These individuals retain social functioning and neurological health.
- They self-identify as needing housing and engage voluntarily with coordinated entry systems.
- The intervention for Pipeline A requires immediate financial intervention, rapid unit placement, and minimal bureaucratic friction.
- This population does not require clinical stabilization.

## Section 3: Pipeline B for Long-Term Encampment Community
- Pipeline B contains approximately sixteen thousand five hundred individuals.
- These individuals have been encamped long enough to develop survival relationships within camp communities.
- Some have psychiatric diagnoses or substance dependence.
- Individuals in Pipeline B can evaluate housing offers and make decisions based on self-interest.
- They refuse existing shelter systems due to forced separation from animal companions, separation from intimate partners, or the threat of losing personal possessions.
- Houston's Assertive Community Treatment program removed these barriers and achieved fifty-eight to seventy percent encampment-level acceptance rates.

## Section 4: Pipeline C for Chronic Psychosis and Clinical Pathways
- Pipeline C contains approximately sixteen thousand individuals.
- This population presents with untreated psychosis lasting five to fifteen years, schizophrenia spectrum disorders, methamphetamine-induced neurological damage, and anosognosia.
- Anosognosia is the neurological inability to perceive one's own psychiatric condition.
- Anosognosia affects fifty to eighty percent of individuals with schizophrenia.
- Frontal lobe damage generates both psychosis and anosognosia.
- Persuasion-based outreach does not work on individuals with anosognosia.
- Pipeline C requires sustained Assertive Community Treatment over twelve to twenty-four months and court-supervised compelled treatment pathways.

## Section 5: Riparian Encampment Sub-Category
- A sub-category of Pipeline C lives in riparian corridors, flood control channels, and storm drains.
- Standard point-in-time counts do not record this population.
- Thermal drone mapping using Forward Looking Infrared sensors identified encampment populations in the LA River corridor, Ballona Creek, and Santa Ana River.
- Encampments in these waterways contaminate them with fecal coliform bacteria at concentrations exceeding Clean Water Act permit standards.
- Contamination provides a legal basis for abatement separate from housing law.
- Globally across all evidence-based programs, the voluntary housing acceptance rate for hidden riparian sub-populations is fifteen to thirty percent.
- UAV thermal imaging maps miles of riparian corridors per flight hour at a low operational cost of several hundred dollars.
- The 2018 Santa Ana River County cleanup serves as the closest operational precedent for coordinated environmental-housing enforcement in California.


## Section 6: Pipeline D for Institutional Rejection and Harm Reduction
- Pipeline D contains approximately two thousand three hundred fifty individuals.
- These individuals choose outdoor living, informal economies, and rejection of institutional structures based on personal preference.
- This population is not clinically destabilized.
- Their street communities provide social bonds, mutual protection, and terrain survival competence.
- The intervention for Pipeline D consists of access to hygiene, nutrition, wound care, and needle exchanges without placement preconditions.
