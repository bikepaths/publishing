# About the Author

Charles J. DiBella has spent three decades working in and observing the systems that serve displaced populations across the United States. His research integrates stress neurobiology, urban economics, commercial real estate analysis, and political philosophy into the Material Dignity Infrastructure model, a full engineering model for addressing chronic unsheltered homelessness through vertically integrated stabilization towers operated as permanent public utilities.

He is the author of *The Architecture of Survival: An Engineering Manual for Human Stabilization Infrastructure* and *The Moral Physics of Survival: Philosophical Foundations for Material Dignity*, the second and third volumes in the MDI series. His working papers on relational dignity, relational engineering, and the polyvagal architecture of outreach have been published through the SSRN research network.

He lives in Los Angeles.
