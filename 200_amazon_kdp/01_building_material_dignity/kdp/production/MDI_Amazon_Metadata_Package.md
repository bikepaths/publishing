**Description (HTML Format for KDP):**
<p><strong>The crisis on our sidewalks is not a mystery. It is a design failure, and design failures have design solutions.</strong></p>

<p>For decades, American cities have responded to chronic unsheltered homelessness with a cycle of temporary street sweeps, warehouse-scale congregate shelters, and isolated apartment placements that lack the relational support necessary for long-term retention. <em>Building Material Dignity</em> provides the engineering alternative.</p>

<p>Drawing on thirty years of direct observation and integrating research from stress neurobiology, urban economics, and commercial real estate, Charles J. DiBella outlines a comprehensive mechanical blueprint to stabilize the unsheltered population. The solution is not behavioral; it is structural. The body requires specific physical conditions to remain functional: temperature regulation, sleep in an acoustically isolated space behind a locked door, and a sustained period of safety long enough for the stress hormones that have saturated the body's tissue to normalize.</p>

<p>This book details the <strong>Material Dignity Infrastructure (MDI)</strong>: a network of vertically integrated stabilization towers acquired from the distressed commercial real estate market and operated as permanent public utilities. You will discover:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>The Mathematics of Collapse</strong>: How the destruction of the kinship buffer and single-room occupancy housing created the modern encampment.</li>
<li><strong>The Open-Air Trauma Ward</strong>: The specific clinical sequence by which street exposure dismantles the brain's capacity for executive function and self-directed behavior.</li>
<li><strong>Phase Zero Decompression</strong>: Why 90 days of uninterrupted sleep behind an STC-65 rated locking door must precede any program or behavioral contract.</li>
<li><strong>The Dunbar Pod</strong>: How limiting residential floor communities to 150 people replicates the biological constraints of human social cognition and drives housing retention.</li>
<li><strong>The Fiscal Reality</strong>: Why building permanent stabilization towers generates an annual efficiency surplus compared to the billions currently spent managing the crisis on the street.</li>
</ul>

<p><em>Building Material Dignity</em> is not an appeal to compassion. It is a manual for action. The buildings are standing. The biology is documented. The budget math works. What remains is the decision to build.</p>
