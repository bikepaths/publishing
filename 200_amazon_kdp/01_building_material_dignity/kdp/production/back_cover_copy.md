The crisis on our sidewalks is not a mystery. It is a design failure, and design failures have design solutions.

For decades, American cities have responded to chronic unsheltered homelessness with a cycle of temporary street sweeps, warehouse-scale congregate shelters, and isolated apartment placements that lack the relational support necessary for long-term retention. Building Material Dignity provides the engineering alternative.

Drawing on thirty years of direct observation and integrating research from stress neurobiology, urban economics, and commercial real estate, Charles J. DiBella outlines a comprehensive mechanical blueprint to stabilize the unsheltered population. The solution is not behavioral; it is structural. The body requires specific physical conditions to remain functional: temperature regulation, sleep in an acoustically isolated space behind a locked door, and a sustained period of safety long enough for the stress hormones that have saturated the body's tissue to normalize.

This book details the Material Dignity Infrastructure (MDI): a network of vertically integrated stabilization towers acquired from the distressed commercial real estate market and operated as permanent public utilities.
