# Chapter 1: The Myth of the Rugged Individual

The American story tells us that everyone should be able to take care of themselves. It teaches the idea of the lone survivor who lives without help from anyone else. The laws of nature tell a different story because an isolated human cannot survive hard times alone. Self-reliance is a luxury that only works when you have a lot of money or hidden support. A person needs a strong network because life is too heavy for one set of shoulders.

When things are going well, a person looks like they are standing on their own. This illusion breaks as soon as a job loss or a health crisis hits the home. In the past, people relied on a safety net made of family and neighbors. We call this the Kinship Buffer since it absorbs the shocks of life. These networks of relatives provided the money and food needed to keep a person from losing their home. They acted as the shock absorbers that prevented the total collapse of the individual.

Many people today see the homeless as a group of behavioral failures. This view ignores the physical laws of survival. The tent on the sidewalk is a sign that a person has run out of support. A single human cannot absorb the impact of a disaster by themselves. When a worker loses a job or suffers a mental break, they must lean on their surroundings. If they have a large bank account, the money absorbs the hit. If they have a strong family, the relatives pool their resources to prevent displacement.

The crisis moves faster when both the money and the family network fail at the same time. An isolated person has no structural weight to hold them in place. Without a bank account and without a family, that person faces immediate street exposure. Living on the sidewalk is a mechanical certainty since a person requires a solid foundation to survive. The encampment is the result of a society that demands people live alone while tearing down the networks that keep them alive. Criticizing the behavior of the poor fails as a solution since survival requires a constant supply of support.
