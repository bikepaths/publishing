# Chapter 8: The Trauma Amplifier

Large shared shelters are the main way cities try to help the homeless. Every year, cities spend billions of dollars on big buildings where hundreds of people sleep in one room. The public thinks these shelters are a kind and helpful solution. The medical truth shows that these buildings make trauma worse. We call this a trauma amplifier because it makes a person’s pain louder instead of helping it go away.

A large shared shelter copies the bad conditions of the street in a different way. A person who enters these buildings must give up almost everything they own. They have to leave in the morning and cannot come back until the evening. They share bathrooms with hundreds of strangers and sleep in rooms without walls or doors. The building follows a strict set of rules that gives the person no choices of their own. This environment takes away the control a person needs to feel safe.

The brain cannot tell the difference between a crowded shelter and a crowded sidewalk. Both places force the body to stay on high alert at all times. Both places deny a person the locking door they need to let their mind rest. Both places cause the lack of sleep that destroys a person’s ability to think. The shelter does not fix the biological damage caused by the street. It keeps that damage alive inside a different kind of box.

Medical facts prove that large shared shelters make mental damage worse. Surviving in these buildings requires following rules that change every day and sleeping in different beds. This total lack of a private space prevents the body from healing. A person needs months of quiet and a locking door to heal from the street whereas they get a loud, stressful building instead. Each stay in a large shelter delays the healing process and deepens the brain damage. The public pays for this Trauma Amplifier at a massive scale every year. We are funding a machine that burns money while physically preventing recovery.
