# Chapter 13: The Five Pipelines

The old system fails because it treats everyone on the street like they have the exact same problem. The machine tries to push every person through a single door. This approach is like using one tool to fix every part of a car engine. The physical truth is that the street population is made up of five very different groups. Each group needs a specific pipeline to get off the sidewalk. When we mix these groups together, the whole system breaks down. 

The first pipeline is for pure economic failure. These people have run out of money and lost their homes. They do not have drug problems or mental illness. They just need a clean room and a locking door. Once they have a safe physical base, they quickly return to work and move back into normal apartments. The old system slows them down by forcing them to attend medical meetings they do not need. 

The second pipeline is for mild health problems. These individuals struggle with early drug use or minor depression because the street has worn them down. They need a quiet room and some basic doctor visits. Given a few months of rest and proper food, their bodies heal and their minds clear. They choose to get better because they still have the power to make good choices. Our towers give them the quiet space they need to rebuild their lives.

The third and fourth pipelines deal with severe physical damage. The third group is locked in deep drug addiction whereas the fourth group suffers from severe mental illness. These people cannot survive in a normal room because their brains are too broken to follow rules. They need locked medical floors where doctors can keep them safe from themselves. The old rules push these sick people into open shelters where they cause chaos for everyone else. A working city must build secure medical tracks for these two groups because voluntary care no longer works for them.

The fifth pipeline is for the predators. A small number of people on the street are true criminals who hide among the weak. They steal tents, sell drugs, and hurt the sick. The city must separate these predators from the victims immediately. This final track is the standard jail system. A safe city cannot allow wolves to live among the sheep. By sorting the street into these five clear pipelines, the new towers give each person the exact physical fix they need.
