# Chapter 13 Atomic Facts

## Section 1: Operating Models and Automation Division
- A two-thousand-unit residential tower operates at ninety percent occupancy.
- Conventional supportive housing facilities allocate operating budgets to paid staff hours for physical plant maintenance, sanitation, and security monitoring.
- The MDI model uses automated systems for functions not requiring judgment, relationship-building, or emotional intelligence, while human staff perform the remaining tasks.

## Section 2: Robotic Cleaning Platform Specifications
- The tower uses the Gausium Scrubber 50 robotic platform for automated cleaning.
- The Gausium Scrubber 50 is an ISO 3741-compliant cleaning unit with eighty percent water recycling efficiency.
- The robotic cleaning platform must be certified under EN IEC 63327 for safety in public spaces.
- Robotic cleaners operate across common circulation corridors, elevator lobbies, and shared social floor surfaces.

- Robotic response to biological waste events requires Pod Steward authorization.
- The Swarm Integration Protocol allows residents to volunteer for a Robotic Swarm Supervision role to shadow and assist the robotic maintenance cycle.
- Resident participation in robotic supervision reduces operating budgets and addresses paranoid threat-perception of automated systems.

## Section 3: Telemetry Data Minimization Protocol
- Sensor telemetry uses a data minimization protocol.
- The private ALMU unit only exports a binary safety signal to the central grid.
- The MDI modular unit features localized edge compute modules that analyze health telemetry within the unit, to prevent raw acoustic or video data from leaving the unit boundary.
- Environmental data, including sound levels, movement patterns, and temperature readings, is processed locally within the unit and purged within twenty-four hours.

- A period of twenty-four consecutive hours with zero detected movement inside a unit triggers a Pod Steward notification for a welfare check.

## Section 4: Pod Steward and Pod Council Functions
- One credentialed peer support specialist with lived experience of homelessness or psychiatric illness serves as Pod Steward for each Dunbar Pod.
- A Dunbar Pod contains one hundred and fifty-four residents.
- The Pod Steward resides in a unit within the pod.
- The Pod Steward knows residents by name, monitors behavioral baselines, and chairs monthly Pod Council meetings.
- Monthly Pod Council meetings allow residents to identify infrastructure concerns, schedule common space use, and mediate social friction.
- The Digital Pod Channel provides opt-in communication that is inaccessible to the Stewardship Authority without resident consent and Board approval.

## Section 5: Measure Alpha Budgeting and Privacy Governance
- Measure Alpha allocates 1.587 percent of its eight-hundred-and-forty-three-million-dollar FY2026-27 proposed budget, which is thirteen million three hundred and eighty thousand dollars annually, to telemetry hardware, robotic maintenance, and cybernetic infrastructure for the pilot tower.
- An independent Privacy Audit Board, which includes resident Pod Council representatives, reviews the data architecture annually.

- The Privacy Audit Board holds veto authority over any expansion of data collection beyond the authorized telemetry substrate.
