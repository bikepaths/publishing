# Chapter 8 Atomic Facts

## Section 1: The Transition Gap
- Placing a person from the street into a permanent apartment delivers a stable environment to a nervous system habituated to threat exposure.
- Enclosed spaces trigger panic in individuals habituated to open environments with visible escape routes.
- Behavioral issues such as stripped copper wiring, invited encampment contacts, and abandoned leases occur when individuals are housed without prior repair sequences.

## Section 2: Phase Zero MHRC Cluster Structure
- Phase Zero is a clinical staging period between street living and permanent housing.
- Phase Zero runs for sixty to ninety days.
- Interventions occur in licensed Mental Health Rehabilitation Center facilities.
- MHRC facilities are deployed as sub-sixteen-unit clusters within the Skid Row zone.
- The sub-sixteen-unit cluster model avoids the federal Institution for Mental Disease Exclusion rule.
- The IMD Exclusion rule disqualifies Medi-Cal billing for psychiatric facilities with more than fifteen beds.
- Each fifteen-bed cluster files an independent licensure application.
- The clusters bill Medi-Cal as sub-acute specialty mental health facilities, qualifying for approximately fifty percent federal matching funds.
- A sixty-day stabilization episode costs approximately fifteen thousand dollars per resident.
- Federal matching funds provide approximately seven thousand five hundred dollars per resident.
- The cluster model requires no new legislation.
- To prevent bottlenecks, the MDI stabilization protocol runs a three-track architecture consisting of a seven to fourteen day rapid track, a thirty to sixty day standard track, and a sixty to one hundred and twenty day extended track.
- The ground floor clinical node maintains a fifteen percent overflow capacity using temporary decompression units.


## Section 3: Metabolic Rehydration and Refeeding Protocols
- The clinical protocol targets four biological systems in a particular sequence.
- Hours zero through four prioritize rehydration using World Health Organization electrolyte solutions.
- Rapid water reintroduction in severely dehydrated individuals with sodium depletion causes hyponatremia or cerebral edema, which can be fatal.
- Hours four through twenty-four introduce nutritional support under the Refeeding Syndrome protocol.
- Rapid reintroduction of carbohydrates in malnourished individuals causes electrolyte shifts leading to cardiac arrhythmia.
- Individuals with a Body Mass Index below sixteen undergo a gradual food reintroduction sequence.

## Section 4: Acoustic Deceleration and Psychiatric Assessment
- Hours twenty-four through seventy-two focus on wound care, infection treatment, and sleep initiation under acoustic isolation.
- Phase Zero modules use a Sound Transmission Class 65 acoustic specification.
- On the street, individuals experience sensory overload from sirens, preventing restorative sleep and causing physiological exhaustion.
- Sensory overload combined with law enforcement contact can cause panic eruptions.
- Acoustic enclosures decelerate the nervous system.
- The psychiatric assessment window opens between days twenty-four and seventy-two.
- Psychiatric evaluations attempted before day twenty-four produce false assessments due to dehydration, malnutrition, and sleep deprivation.

## Section 5: Measurable Functional Exit Indicators
- Exit from Phase Zero requires meeting at least two of five measurable functional indicators within the sixty to ninety day window.
- The five functional indicators consist of sustained hygiene maintenance without staff prompting, six or more hours of uninterrupted sleep without pharmacological support, consistent orientation to person, place, and date, participation in at least one structured group activity per day, and independent initiation of at least one daily living task.
- Meeting these indicators marks readiness for a residential lease.
