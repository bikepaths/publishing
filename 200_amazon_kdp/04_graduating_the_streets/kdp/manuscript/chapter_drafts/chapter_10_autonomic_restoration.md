# Chapter 10: Autonomic Restoration

With his social anxiety under control, Marcus began to rebuild his daily autonomy. Recovery requires moving from passive assistance to self-determination. To support this growth, the program offered micro-enterprise opportunities inside the building to help residents learn trade skills. Marcus joined the bicycle workshop. 

Working in the shop gave him a structured morning routine. Every morning at nine o'clock, he walked down to the basement workshop to organize tools and assemble metal parts. Hands-on labor kept his mind focused on physical materials. As he mastered the welding process, his confidence grew, proving his ability to contribute. 

Marcus earned small stipends for his work in the shop. With this income, he opened his first bank account in five years. Managing his own money restored a feeling of independence that he had not experienced since living on the streets. For Buster, Marcus bought special dog treats with his first earnings. 

Autonomic restoration occurs when a person regains control over their daily schedule and choices. Through productive micro-enterprise labor, the program helped Marcus transition from a client receiving aid to a contributor. This physical agency restored his self-efficacy. When a person controls their own work, they rebuild their path back to society.
