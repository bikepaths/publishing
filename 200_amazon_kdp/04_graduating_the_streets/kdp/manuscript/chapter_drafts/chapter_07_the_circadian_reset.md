# Chapter 7: The Circadian Reset

Following a week of deep sleep, Marcus began to reset his biological clock. Living in the dark riverbed had forced him to stay awake at night to guard against violence. To restore his internal clock, the stabilization unit featured large east-facing windows that flooded his room with early morning sunlight. This natural light woke him without alarms. 

During the afternoon, Marcus sat outside on the sunlit terrace with Buster. Sunlight warmed his skin and regulated his body temperature after months of exposure to cold riverbed fog. As his skin absorbed the bright morning light, his endocrine system rebuilt the hormones needed for daytime alertness and energy. Nearby, Buster ran on the grass. 

As evening approached, the indoor lighting adjusted to match the setting sun. In place of harsh fluorescent bulbs, the building used low-intensity amber lights in the corridors and bedrooms. This warm lighting allowed his brain to produce melatonin naturally. By removing artificial blue light, the environment supported his nervous system in transitioning to rest. 

Within two weeks, Marcus slept and woke at consistent hours. Deep, restorative sleep replaced his nighttime panic. Aligning his body with the natural cycles of light and dark restored his endocrine health. This physical reset built the foundation for his cognitive recovery.
