Universal dignity rests on human nature rather than performance or functional capacity. Below the biological floor, choice and consent cease to operate.

For decades, political philosophies and municipal programs have assumed that unsheltered individuals possess intact cognitive agency. Thirty years of street-level observation and neurobiological research prove otherwise. The Moral Physics of Survival provides the ethical and empirical framework to justify direct, systemic intervention.

Charles J. DiBella outlines the moral necessity of the Material Dignity Infrastructure (MDI). When street exposure, sleep deprivation, and chronic trauma destroy prefrontal cortex function, biological recovery must precede any housing choice or behavioral contract. We are morally obligated to protect the vulnerable through compelled clinical intervention and public utility design. This is not an abstract theory; it is an empirical science mapping the causal mechanisms of municipal collapse.
