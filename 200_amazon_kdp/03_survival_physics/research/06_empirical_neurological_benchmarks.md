# 06_EMPIRICAL_NEUROLOGICAL_BENCHMARKS: Field Data and Clinical Foundations

This document compiles empirical metrics, clinical models, and policy parameters to support the manuscript chapters of The Moral Physics of Survival.

## 1. Neurophysiological States under Polyvagal Theory

The human nervous system operates across three distinct biological states. Street conditions alter these states progressively.

### Calm-Engaged State
- Physiological markers: Deep breathing; relaxed facial muscles; sustained eye contact.
- Cognitive capacity: Verbal processing; future planning; option evaluation.
- Intervention requirement: Calm engagement is the only state where a person can evaluate and accept a housing offer.

### Mobilized Threat State
- Physiological markers: Accelerated chest breathing; elevated adrenaline; constant scanning.
- Cognitive capacity: Narrowed survival focus; impaired deliberation; autonomic flight or fight response.
- Intervention requirement: Workers must provide physical distance and patience to allow the hormonal cycle to subside before introducing offers.

### Dorsal-Vagal Shutdown State
- Physiological markers: Vacant gaze; collapsed posture; complete immobility.
- Cognitive capacity: Stalled language processing; metabolic energy conservation; offline executive function.
- Intervention requirement: Workers must deliver quiet declarative statements without questions; the system must not record shutdown as a behavioral refusal.

## 2. Fiscal and Economic Metrics

The fiscal case for transition infrastructure relies on verified municipal cost data.

### Baseline Municipal Expenses
- Street management cost: $50,000 per chronically unsheltered person annually in Los Angeles County.
- Covered services: Emergency department visits; psychiatric holds; law enforcement contacts; recurring sanitation sweeps.

### Cost Avoidance Outcomes
- Dennis Culhane in a 2002 cohort study: Formerly homeless individuals placed in supportive housing produced over $16,000 per person annually in avoided public service costs.
- HUD permanent supportive housing study: Heavy service users produced cost savings exceeding $46,000 per person annually.
- California State Auditor in 2024: The state allocated 24,000,000,000 dollars to homelessness programs between 2018 and 2024; poor tracking and coordination allowed the unsheltered population to grow.

## 3. Statutory and Policy Context

The operational environment in 2026 demands compliance with shifting legal and funding structures.

### Statutory Environment
- California AB 2011 and AB 2243: Provide by-right administrative approval for commercial-to-residential housing conversions, bypassing municipal discretionary veto points.
- Grants Pass v. Johnson in 2024: The Supreme Court authorized public space camping bans even without available shelter beds.
- American Journal of Public Health in 2026: Oakland encampment sweeps more than doubled post-Grants Pass, causing loss of medical equipment and disconnecting residents from clinics.

### Federal Funding Shifts
- Budget proposals: A proposed 44 percent reduction in HUD programs combined with an 84 percent cut to program administration staffing.
- Policy guidelines: Federal guidelines replaced Housing First models with Treatment First mandates, prioritizing street outreach and short-term shelters over permanent housing.
- Veteran success benchmark: Veteran homelessness declined by 56 percent between 2010 and 2026, dropping from 74,000 to 32,500 due to targeted, multi-year program funding.

## 4. Material Dignity Operational Principles

The pipeline executes stabilization through three non-discretionary protocols.

### Biological State Outreach
- Assessment: Workers read posture, breathing, and eye tracking rather than compliance history.
- Timeline: Outreach requires three to twelve weekly contacts to transition a person from shutdown to calm engagement.

### Non-Expiring Offers
- Trust accumulation: The housing offer has no expiration window; trust builds on biological timelines rather than administrative calendars.

### Social Network Preservation
- Network mapping: Workers administer a four-question survey in under five minutes to map gear-watching, food-sharing, crisis reliance, and toxicity flags.
- Placement: Cohorts of twelve transition together to preserve verified safety bonds and separate identified risks.
- International benchmark: Finland's Y-Foundation reports an 82 percent two-year tenancy retention rate using similar congregate-to-independent sequencing.
