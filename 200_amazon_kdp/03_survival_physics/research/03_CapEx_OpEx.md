## Operational Expenditure and Efficiency Surplus Payback

### Baseline Street Costs

The municipal baseline cost to manage 2,000 chronically unsheltered individuals on the street is $100,000,000 annually. This equates to $50,000 per person each year.

### MDI Operational Performance Projections

The capital recovery of the prototype project relies on the municipal efficiency surplus across three performance cases.

#### 1. Best Case Scenario
- Gross savings: $100,000,000
- Net recurring expenses: $18,000,000
- Efficiency Surplus: $82,000,000
- Capital recovery timeline: 29 months

#### 2. Most Probable Case
- Gross savings: $75,000,000
- Net recurring expenses: $22,500,000
- Efficiency Surplus: $52,500,000
- Capital recovery timeline: 45 months

#### 3. Worst Case Scenario
- Gross savings: $60,000,000
- Net recurring expenses: $27,000,000
- Efficiency Surplus: $33,000,000
- Capital recovery timeline: 72 months

### Hardening Cost Integration

Operating expenses include specific allocations to prevent operational failure. The billing leakage reserve requires $1,650,000 annually to offset documentation loss. The autonomous administration ecosystem requires $1,200,000 annually for dedicated billing and accounting teams. The socio-educational residency program requires $800,000 annually to deploy volunteer interns under twelve-month commitments. The somatic maintenance escrow requires $500,000 annually for immediate modular component replacement.
