### Staff Qualifications

The clinical lead requires a board-certified psychiatry license and DEA registration. Clinical personnel must hold a California professional license such as Licensed Clinical Social Worker, Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist, or Registered Nurse, along with Crisis Intervention Team training. Professional advocates require a Master of Social Work degree and trauma-informed engagement training. Peer specialists require certification through the California Peer Support Specialist program, combined with documented lived experience of homelessness, addiction, or psychiatric recovery. Operations and technical staff must maintain industry-specific credentials such as Pharmacy Technician Certification, ServSafe Manager certification, or CompTIA network credentials.
