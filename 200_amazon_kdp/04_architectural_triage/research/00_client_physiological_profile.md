# Client Physiological Profile: Marcus

Case study tracking clinical indicators of client extracted from high-acuity waterway environment.

## 1. Field Background and Encampment Location
*   **Subject**: Marcus, age 47.
*   **Duration of Untreated Homelessness**: 9 years unsheltered.
*   **Field Location**: Los Angeles Riverbed, Glendale Narrows, dense willow grove island adjacent to Bowtie Parcel (34.1165° N, 118.2615° W).
*   **Taxonomy Classification**: Pipeline C (behaviorally calcified chronic, history of total service resistance).
*   **Environmental Exposure**: Wooded riverbed island, highly isolated, damp clay soil, subject to seasonal flash flooding and damp thermal conditions.

## 2. Somatic and Physiological Presentation at Intake
*   **Bilateral Immersion Foot (Trench Foot)**: Severe tissue maceration across plantar surfaces. Secondary bacterial cellulitis visible. Pain drove extreme mobility limitation.
*   **Severe Malnourishment**: Body Mass Index (BMI) recorded at 15.8. Muscle wasting evident across limbs.
*   **Respiratory Pathology**: Sub-acute bronchitis resulting from prolonged damp exposure and campfire smoke inhalation.
*   **Sympathetic Nervous System Overdrive**: Salivary cortisol levels three times higher than healthy baselines. Absence of pupillary regulation under variable lighting.
*   **Sleep Fragmentation**: Client slept in 90-to-120 minute intervals due to survival vigilance against rising waters and predation.
*   **Neurological State**: Deep dorsal-vagal shutdown. Non-verbal on approach, avoiding eye contact, displaying complete flat affect. Sympathetic overdrive blocked executive functioning.
