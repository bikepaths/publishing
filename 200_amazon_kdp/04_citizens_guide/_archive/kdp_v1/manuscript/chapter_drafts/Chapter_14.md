# Chapter 14: The Phase Zero Intake

Standard municipal intake systems guarantee immediate failure. A typical shelter protocol requires a person entering from the street to complete a twenty-page administrative form. The facility expects a human being experiencing active psychosis and severe sleep deprivation to execute complex data entry. The individual must navigate behavioral rules, sign liability waivers, and attend mandatory orientation meetings. This process weaponizes cognitive load against a failing nervous system. Administrative compliance requires intact executive function. Demanding administrative compliance from a brain in metabolic collapse guarantees institutional rejection. 

Material Dignity Infrastructure rewrites this protocol through the Phase Zero framework. Phase Zero dictates that the first seventy-two hours of stabilization require zero administrative compliance. When an individual enters a Dunbar Pod, the system asks nothing of them. The facility does not request signatures. Staff members do not conduct clinical interviews. The individual faces zero behavioral ultimatums. The system exclusively provides the environment for the resident to sleep, consume calories, and regulate core temperature. 

This protocol recognizes a strict physiological hierarchy. A clinical baseline cannot exist until a biological baseline exists. A psychiatrist cannot accurately assess a schizophrenia diagnosis while the patient operates on severe sleep deprivation. Starvation induces acute paranoia. Hypothermia degrades cognitive mapping. The system must eliminate the immediate metabolic deficits before attempting any higher-order psychiatric evaluation. The first three days serve exclusively to arrest physical collapse. 

Following the seventy-two-hour stabilization window, the individual transitions to Phase One. Phase One initiates the actual triage process. The resident begins engaging with case managers and participating in pod governance. MDI enforces a strict temporal boundary for this phase. The system requires a thirty-day minimum stay to permit structural neurobiological repair. Short-term shelters fail because they attempt to repair compounding trauma in forty-eight hours. Neurological networks require weeks to map a new, secure environment. 

Progression through the triage pipeline relies on empirical data rather than arbitrary timelines. MDI utilizes a forty percent indicator completion rule. A resident must achieve forty percent of their customized stabilization milestones before the system clears them for independent housing or lateral transfer. These milestones track sleep continuity, caloric intake, and reduced cortisol markers. 

By eliminating the initial administrative burden, Phase Zero protects the fragile entry point of clinical stabilization. The system meets the resident at their biological reality. The infrastructure absorbs the cognitive load so the resident can begin the mechanical process of physical healing.
