# Chapter 15: Metabolic Stabilization

Thirty days represents the absolute minimum stabilization duration. This timeline does not exist for administrative convenience. It exists because of neurobiology. The human brain requires sustained, unbroken relief from the stress cascade before measurable prefrontal cortex recovery begins. Cortisol reduction dictates the timeline. The literature demonstrates that early cognitive improvement requires consistent sleep, dense nutrition, and absolute physical safety over multiple weeks. A damaged nervous system cannot rebuild neural pathways in forty-eight hours. Short-term municipal shelters fail because they attempt to compress a biological healing process into an impossible administrative window. 

Material Dignity Infrastructure defines metabolic stabilization through five specific clearance indicators. The clinical team monitors sustained personal hygiene, sleep continuity exceeding six hours, consistent orientation to time and place, active group participation, and independent execution of daily living activities. The infrastructure does not demand perfection. MDI utilizes a provisional forty percent threshold. When a resident achieves two of the five indicators, the system initiates a clearance review. Requiring perfect compliance creates an institutional trap. The forty percent rule functions as an initial calibration parameter rather than an absolute clinical standard. 

Executing this clearance review requires precise temporal data. MDI enforces a strict telemetry requirement. A clearance assessment must rely on clinical data collected within the previous twenty-four hours. If the data exceeds this window, the system flags it as stale. Stale data automatically triggers a mandatory human override. The system prevents automated algorithms from executing triage decisions based on outdated biological information. 

Clinical clearance does not signify graduation from the system. It represents an internal transition. When a resident achieves the stabilization metrics, they transfer from the intensive Phase Zero intake unit into a standard residential Dunbar Pod. The clinical relationship continues. The supportive infrastructure continues. Only the intensity of the immediate environment changes. The resident enters an environment demanding higher social engagement while offering greater personal autonomy. 

Critically, the protocol defines what clearance does not require. Clearance does not require vocational engagement. It does not demand absolute sobriety. It does not require the complete resolution of underlying psychiatric diagnoses. Clearance remains an exclusively biological metric rather than a behavioral metric. The system simply verifies that the individual possesses the physiological stability required to participate in communal living. The infrastructure secures the biological floor before asking the resident to climb the social ladder.
