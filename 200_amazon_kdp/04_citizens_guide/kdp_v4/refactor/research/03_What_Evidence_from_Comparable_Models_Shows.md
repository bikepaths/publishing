# Pre-Compositional Research: Evidence from Comparable Models

## 1. Finland's Y-Foundation (Housing First / *Asunto Ensin*)
*   **What it does:** Replaced temporary emergency shelters with permanent, independent rental apartments accompanied by unconditional, on-site support services. Housing is guaranteed regardless of sobriety or "life readiness."
*   **Outcome data:** Reduced long-term homelessness by over 50% since 2008. Converted traditional shelters entirely. Generates societal savings of ~€15,000 per person per year through reduced emergency healthcare, policing, and justice system utilization.
*   **MDI Elements Supported:** Validates the core assertion that physiological and material stability must precede behavioral rehabilitation. Proves that providing unconditional infrastructure is cheaper than managing chronic street attrition.
*   **Where MDI Extends Beyond:** Y-Foundation operates primarily in the voluntary housing domain. MDI integrates compelled Phase Zero stabilization for anosognosic individuals (Pipeline C), a higher-acuity cohort that Housing First models often struggle to retain without specialized neuro-architectural containment.

## 2. Medical Respite and Recuperative Care (NIMRC)
*   **What it does:** Provides short-term, residential, person-centered support for individuals experiencing homelessness who are too ill to recover on the streets but do not meet the threshold for acute hospital admission.
*   **Outcome data:** Dramatically reduces future emergency department visits and hospital readmissions. Lowers hospital length-of-stay, saving hospital systems millions annually. Serves as a critical bridge connecting individuals to primary care and long-term housing.
*   **MDI Elements Supported:** Validates the efficacy of intermediate, specialized stabilization environments. Proves that treating acute vulnerability outside traditional hospital or shelter environments yields superior clinical outcomes.
*   **Where MDI Extends Beyond:** Medical respite is typically short-term (weeks) and primarily focused on somatic illness/injury. MDI's Phase Zero requires a minimum 30-day baseline specifically targeted at prefrontal cortex regeneration and psychiatric restabilization, integrating continuous neuro-architectural parameters (e.g., STC 65).

## 3. Assertive Community Treatment (ACT) Teams
*   **What it does:** Deploys highly integrated, multidisciplinary teams (psychiatry, nursing, social work) directly into the community ("in vivo") to support individuals with severe and persistent mental illness (SPMI) and comorbid substance use.
*   **Outcome data:** Meta-analyses show ACT reduces homelessness by 37% and psychiatric symptom severity by 26% compared to standard care. Significantly decreases psychiatric inpatient days and emergency department utilization while increasing housing stability.
*   **MDI Elements Supported:** Validates the necessity of intensive, multidisciplinary, unbroken continuity of care for the Pipeline C and D populations. Proves that standard outpatient models fail this demographic.
*   **Where MDI Extends Beyond:** ACT teams operate within the existing fragmented infrastructure (streets, scattered-site housing). MDI physically centralizes the ACT equivalent within the Dunbar Pod, fusing the clinical team directly into the built environment rather than dispatching them to disparate, uncontrolled locations.

## 4. Inpatient Psychiatric Stabilization
*   **What it does:** Provides acute, locked stabilization for individuals experiencing severe psychiatric crises, often under involuntary holds (e.g., 5150/5250).
*   **Outcome data:** Homelessness severely complicates inpatient care, extending length of stay by up to 27.5 days due to "bed blocking" (inability to safely discharge to the street). Discharging homeless patients back to the street results in massive 30- and 90-day readmission spikes and continuous ED utilization.
*   **MDI Elements Supported:** Validates the systemic failure of the current pipeline. Proves that without contiguous step-down infrastructure, acute psychiatric stabilization is immediately undone by street exposure.
*   **Where MDI Extends Beyond:** Traditional inpatient units are clinical hospitals focused on immediate crisis abatement. MDI’s Phase Zero shifts the paradigm from clinical crisis management to structural capability restoration, functioning as the missing contiguous step-down bridge that prevents the street-to-hospital cycle.
