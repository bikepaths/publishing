# Pre-Compositional Research: Capability-to-Specification Translation

## 1. Literature Base for Physical and Clinical Conditions

### A. Cognitive and Social Limits (Dunbar)
- **Dunbar (1992):** *"Neocortex size as a constraint on group size in primates."* Establishes the cognitive limit for maintaining stable, reciprocal relationships at ~150 individuals. This forms the basis for the "Dunbar Pod" maximum resident capacity.
- **Dunbar (2021):** Reaffirms the 150 limit as a biologically constrained fractal layer of human social networks, grounded in prefrontal cortex capacity and endorphin system functionality. Validates the structural boundary of Phase Zero stabilization pods to prevent cognitive overload.

### B. Acoustic Isolation and Sleep Architecture
- **Psychiatric Inpatient Design Standards:** Facility Guidelines Institute (FGI) mandates high-performance acoustic isolation (STC 50-60+) for patient privacy and low-stimulation environments. STC 65 provides extreme attenuation to prevent sensory overload in hyper-aroused states, shielding the sleep environment from external triggers.
- **Physical Constraints:** STC 65 walls require double-studding, staggered studs, and multi-layered gypsum, satisfying both anti-ligature safety requirements and extreme acoustic buffering necessary for recovery.

### C. Prefrontal Cortex Recovery (Walker & Arnsten)
- **Walker (2017) - *Why We Sleep*:** Sleep deprivation severs functional connectivity between the prefrontal cortex (executive control) and the amygdala (emotional reactivity). Restoring this connection requires uninterrupted, high-quality recovery sleep.
- **Arnsten (2009):** *"Stress signalling pathways that impair prefrontal cortex structure and function."* Chronic stress triggers signaling pathways (cAMP-PKA) that disconnect PFC neural networks, leading to dendritic atrophy. Reversing this structural damage requires a sustained period (e.g., 30 days) of biological safety and low catecholamine release to rebuild top-down executive control.
