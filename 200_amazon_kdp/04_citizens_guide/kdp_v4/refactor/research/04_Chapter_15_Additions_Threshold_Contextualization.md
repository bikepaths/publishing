# Pre-Compositional Research: Threshold Contextualization (Chapter 15 Additions)

## 1. Contextualizing the 40% Clearance Threshold
The 40% clearance threshold for Phase Zero graduation is a provisional engineering parameter, not a universally validated psychiatric metric. It requires contextualization against established clinical tools used to measure functional independence and Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) in severe psychiatric populations:

*   **Barthel Index & Functional Independence Measure (FIM):** While foundational in rehabilitation medicine for assessing basic ADLs (mobility, self-care), these tools primarily capture physical deficits. The FIM adds cognitive and social dimensions, but both frequently fail to capture the severe executive dysfunction characteristic of anosognosia and chronic street trauma without supplementary cognitive screening.
*   **UCSD Performance-Based Skills Assessment (UPSA):** Designed specifically for severe mental illnesses (e.g., schizophrenia), the UPSA measures Instrumental ADLs (finance, communication, comprehension/planning) through standardized role-play. It is highly predictive of independent living capacity. The MDI 40% threshold effectively functions as a continuous, ambient equivalent to the UPSA—measuring *manifest performance* in a controlled environment rather than isolated *functional capacity* during an exam.
*   **Step-Down Clearance Criteria Analogies:** In medical respite and psychiatric Intensive Outpatient Programs (IOPs), clearance is predicated on stabilization of acute symptoms, achievement of documented clinical goals, and confirmed placement. MDI formalizes these subjective clinical decisions into an objective, data-driven threshold.

## 2. Nested RCT Validation Design
Because the 40% metric is provisional, MDI integrates a continuous validation mechanism using a **Nested Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT)** design:
*   **Design Structure:** Partially nested design where patient cohorts within specific Dunbar Pods act as clusters. 
*   **Outcome Measures:** Primary outcomes include 90-day housing retention post-clearance, 30-day psychiatric readmission rates, and cross-validation of MDI system data against independent, clinician-administered UPSA-Brief and FIM scores.
*   **Sample Size & ICC:** Requires a statistically significant sample size adjusted for the Intracluster Correlation Coefficient (ICC) of the specific Dunbar Pods, ensuring variance driven by specific pod dynamics does not skew the validation of the 40% metric itself.
*   **Timeline:** Initial feasibility validation at 6 months post-activation; full threshold validation requires a minimum 24-month longitudinal study to capture post-discharge durability.

## 3. Calibration Period Governance
Until the nested RCT validates or adjusts the 40% threshold, the system operates in a strict calibration period to prevent premature or delayed clearances:
*   **Override Authority:** Only the Chief Medical Officer (or designated Lead Psychiatrist) of the facility holds override authority to discharge a resident who has not met the threshold, or to retain a resident who has.
*   **Documentation Requirement:** Any override requires a formal, peer-reviewed clinical justification logged in the patient's electronic health record, explicitly detailing why the systemic data contradicts clinical judgment.
*   **Review Schedule:** The multidisciplinary clinical team (psychiatry, nursing, social work) must review threshold data against actual patient presentation weekly during Phase Zero. Aggregate override data must be reviewed monthly by the facility's ethics and clinical governance board to adjust the threshold algorithm if systemic bias is detected.
