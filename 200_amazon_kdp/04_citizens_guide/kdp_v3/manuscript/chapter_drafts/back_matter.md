## A Note on Sources

All technical claims in this book are sourced in the academic paper by Charles J. DiBella, "The Moral Physics of Survival: Thermodynamic Precarity, Cognitive Agency, and the Ethics of Compelled Stabilization," which contains the full bibliography. Key sources for citizen readers seeking to go deeper include:

On sleep neuroscience: Matthew Walker's *Why We Sleep* (2017) is the single most accessible and comprehensive account of sleep science available to general readers. It is the primary source for the sleep specification claims in Chapter 6.

On stress neuroscience: Robert Sapolsky's *Why Zebras Don't Get Ulcers* is the standard accessible text on the HPA axis, cortisol, and the neurological consequences of chronic stress. Bruce McEwen's peer-reviewed work, particularly his 2017 paper in *Chronic Stress*, is the primary academic source.

On prefrontal cortex degradation: Amy Arnsten's 2009 paper in *Nature Reviews Neuroscience* on stress signalling pathways is the foundational academic source. For a general audience, Sapolsky's work covers the same material in accessible form.

On Housing First: Sam Tsemberis, Leyla Gulcur, and Maria Nakae's 2004 paper in the *American Journal of Public Health* remains the definitive study of Housing First outcomes.

On the capability approach: Amartya Sen's *Development as Freedom* (1999) and Martha Nussbaum's *Creating Capabilities* (2011) are both readable by a general audience and provide the philosophical foundation for the sequential resolution model.

On spatial exclusion: Don Mitchell's 1997 paper in *Antipode* on the annihilation of space by anti-homeless laws is the foundational text in critical geography on this topic.

On homelessness sociology: Teresa Gowan's *Hobos, Hustlers, and Backsliders* (2010) is the most rigorous ethnographic account of how unsheltered people actually experience and navigate the service system.

On Defensible Space Theory: Oscar Newman's *Defensible Space* (1972) is the foundational text and remains highly readable.

On Routine Activity Theory: Lawrence Cohen and Marcus Felson's 1979 paper in the *American Sociological Review* established the theory that shapes the MDI approach to encampment predation.

## Open Source Tools for Citizen Analysis

The following tools are freely available and require no license fees:

QGIS — qgis.org — Geographic information system for spatial analysis and mapping

Python — python.org — Programming language with extensive libraries for data analysis (pandas, geopandas, matplotlib, scikit-learn)

OpenStreetMap — openstreetmap.org — Freely available geographic data for any location

HUD Data Portal — hudexchange.info/programs/hdx/ — Annual homelessness count data, CoC spending data, housing inventory counts

Census Bureau American Community Survey — census.gov/acs — Economic, demographic, and housing data at the census tract level

LA County Assessor Portal — assessor.lacounty.gov — Property ownership, assessed value, and vacancy data (and equivalent portals in other counties)
