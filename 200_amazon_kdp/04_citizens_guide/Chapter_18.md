# Chapter 18: Upward Mobility

Material Dignity Infrastructure does not exist to provide permanent clinical housing for every resident. The system exists to restore cognitive and civic agency. For a specific population experiencing profound neurological damage, restored agency means a lifetime within a supported community environment. For others, restored agency means a complete transition to scattered-site independent housing. The system defines both outcomes as absolute successes. MDI operates as a mechanism for generating capacity rather than a final destination. 

The infrastructure generates this capacity through a structured upward mobility pathway. Residents progress sequentially through the pipeline categories as their biological stability increases. A C-Riparian resident transitions to the C-Calcified or B-Engageable cohort immediately upon extraction from a wetland. A C-Calcified resident transitions to the B-Engageable cohort after twelve months of sustained stabilization resolves their anosognosia. A B-Engageable resident advances to the A-Voluntary cohort when they establish economic readiness. 

Economic readiness does not require full employment. It does not require total financial independence. Economic readiness requires internal conversion capacity. This specific capacity includes the cognitive function, social connection, and practical skills necessary to utilize standard municipal housing resources. 

MDI actively manufactures this economic readiness through the Gardien pipeline. The system trains stabilized residents to operate the facility. Peer support specialization represents a credentialed profession generating real income. Pod stewardship builds direct management skills. Cafe stewardship builds daily routine and reinforces a structured work identity. The infrastructure pays residents to run the infrastructure. This internal micro-economy bridges clinical stabilization and external employment. 

When an A-Voluntary resident achieves economic readiness, the system executes a transition to scattered-site housing. The facility connects the resident directly to the municipal Coordinated Entry System and standard economic support networks. The clinical relationship does not terminate upon physical exit. It shifts to a lighter-touch outreach model. Case managers continue monitoring the individual in their independent environment to prevent cyclical collapse. 

At the population level, success does not produce an empty stabilization tower. Success produces a flowing pipeline. A functional system continuously moves people from acute crisis, through biological stabilization, into vocational readiness, and finally toward independent transition. The stabilization tower operates as permanent public infrastructure for the next cohort entering the triage pipeline. Building this infrastructure guarantees that a municipality possesses the mechanical capacity to end chronic street exposure permanently.
