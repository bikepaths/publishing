# 03_FIELD_ARCHITECTURE: The Pre-Admission Street-Level Pipeline
## Material Dignity Infrastructure — Upstream Engagement and Encampment Resolution
*Feeds: The upstream operational section of the manuscript. This is the system that feeds the tower.*

---

### Overview

The MDI tower is a terminal infrastructure node. It cannot function as an intake mechanism for Pipeline C (behaviorally calcified chronic) without a pre-admission field architecture operating at the encampment level 12-24 months in advance. The Field Architecture is the pipeline. Without it, the tower houses Pipelines A and B while Pipeline C remains on the street, congregating at the base of the building.

The Houston "The Way Home" model and Finland's Housing First national program both demonstrate that the operational prerequisite for clearing streets permanently is a by-name, real-time registry matched to specific housing inventory — not a generic referral system matched to a waitlist.

---

### Phase 1: The By-Name HMIS Registry (Months 1-6, Pre-Operations)

**Objective**: Create a named, mapped profile of every chronic encampment individual in the target corridor before any tower unit is offered.

**Mechanics**:
- Deploy field documentation teams to all encampment sites within the MDI target zone.
- For each individual: record known name or identifier, GPS-mapped location pattern (fixed vs. nomadic), observable survival anchors (pet species/name, partner/companion, primary possessions), tentative Pipeline classification (A/B/C), and behavioral observation notes.
- Enter all data into a Homeless Management Information System (HMIS) instance managed by the MDI Stewardship Authority — not shared across fragmented agency networks that lose data at handoffs.
- The by-name list is the operational backbone. Every housing unit pre-opening is pre-matched to a named individual on this list. The warm offer is specific because the preparation was specific.

**Houston Precedent**: HMIS integration across 100+ partner agencies was the single factor most cited in Houston's 60% reduction. Real-time data eliminated the "coordination lag" where individuals fell through agency handoffs.

---

### Phase 2: ACT Team Sustained Presence (Months 3-24, Parallel to Registry)

**Team-Level Identity and Redundant Coverage**:
High sector turnover—historically 20-30% annually—is a primary cause of relationship failure in chronic outreach. To maintain operational consistency, the MDI standard mandates a 20 percent staffing buffer and a **team-level identity protocol** where two or more staff members share relationship responsibility for every resident. This redundant coverage prevents trust cycles from resetting when individual staff members transition; consequently, the relationship with the participant remains a property of the system rather than the individual clinician.

**Team Composition (per encampment cluster)**:
- 1 Psychiatrist (mobile, not clinic-based)
- 1 Registered Nurse
- 1 Licensed Social Worker
- 1 Outreach Case Manager
- 1 Peer Support Specialist (lived experience — critical for trust-building)
- 1 Substance Use Counselor
- 1 Team Coordinator

**Operational Protocol**:
- Do not lead with service offers. Lead with presence. Coffee, food, wound care, harm reduction supplies — no intake forms, no conditions.
- Establish a fixed schedule: same individuals at the same encampment on the same days. Predictability reduces threat-perception in paranoid individuals over time.
- HMIS notes updated after every contact: behavioral changes, new anchors identified, crisis windows observed.
- The peer specialist's function is irreplaceable. Pipeline C individuals with paranoid systems can often accept contact from someone who has visibly survived the same experiences. The psychiatrist and social worker follow the peer specialist's relationship, not the reverse.

**Engagement Timeline Realities**:
- Pipeline B: 4-16 weeks of contact before warm offer is productively received.
- Pipeline C: 6-24 months. No ceiling. Some individuals require multi-year sustained engagement. The ACT literature is clear: persistence without pressure is the only evidence-based approach for this cohort.
- The team does not discharge an individual for refusal. Refusal is noted. Contact continues.

---

### Phase 3: The Warm Offer Window — Execution Protocol

**Timing**: Voluntary movement from street to housing most frequently occurs in a 48-72 hour window following an acute crisis event at the encampment site. Death of a nearby individual, violent incident, severe weather event, or acute medical emergency. The window closes. ACT teams who maintain sustained presence capture it. One-time outreach teams miss it entirely.

**The Offer Structure (specific, immediate, irreversible)**:
- Unit assignment pre-confirmed in HMIS before the conversation begins.
- "Tower 1, Floor 14, Pod 3, Unit 14-07 has your name on it."
- "Biscuit [the dog] has a kennel unit at the veterinary node, three doors from your unit."
- "Your cart will be photographed, tagged, and stored in the secure possession facility on site. You can access it any time."
- "We can go now."

The specificity of this offer is not rhetorical. It is structural. It eliminates every rational objection Pipeline B uses and reduces the cognitive load on Pipeline C individuals who cannot process abstract future states.

**Document-Ready Protocol**: Social worker prepares all intake documentation (ID, benefits enrollment stubs, housing agreement) before the offer conversation. When the yes arrives, bureaucratic delay — historically the primary cause of recanted agreements — is eliminated. Movement happens within hours.

---

### Phase 4: Encampment Resolution (Post-Housing Placement Only)

**The Leaf Blower Effect**: What the MDI framework terms the Leaf Blower Effect — encampment clearance without simultaneous housing placement produces geographic redistribution only. Municipal sweeps that do not follow the by-name registry and warm offer protocol are counterproductive to the MDI mission — they sever ACT relationships and extend engagement timelines by months.

**The MDI Protocol**:
- No encampment site is cleared until every individual at that site has been offered a specific, immediate unit or is actively under CARE Court/AOT supervision (see `04_legal_lever_system.md`).
- Clearance is a logistics event, not a law enforcement event. Sanitation and storage teams accompany, not precede, the housing placement.
- Post-clearance follow-up: ACT team maintains contact with placed individuals for minimum 90 days to prevent re-encampment.

PIT methodology systematically undercounts the most deeply hidden encampments (storm drains, rail corridors, canyon sites, bridge understructures), which skew disproportionately toward Pipeline C. Actual Pipeline C population is likely higher than modeled below.

### Workforce Funding Stack

The MDI field workforce is not funded from a single appropriation. It draws from four interlocking revenue streams, with Medi-Cal reimbursement as the load-bearing base.

**1. Medi-Cal Billable Services (Primary Revenue Stream)**
ACT is a Medi-Cal-billable service in California under the Community Mental Health Services framework. Approximately 85-90% of the Pipeline C population qualifies for Medi-Cal. At the standard ACT billing rate (~$150-190/day per enrolled client), a single team carrying 100 clients generates:
- ~$5.5M-$6.9M annually in Medi-Cal reimbursement
- Across 130 teams: **~$715M-$897M annually in federal-state insurance offset**

**Billing Integrity and Leakage Resilience**:
The fiscal model anticipates imperfect capture. While 85-90% of Pipeline C is Medi-Cal eligible, the administrative overhead of documentation and eligibility lapses typically produces a 20-30% billing leakage. The MDI Stewardship Authority employs a dedicated **Billing Integrity Team** to track eligibility in real-time, while the Efficiency Surplus (see `09_fiscal_architecture.md`) serves as a fallback funding buffer to ensure clinical staffing levels remain constant during reimbursement gaps. This funding acts as a recurring, federal-state matched, politically durable health insurance reimbursement mechanism.

**2. MHSA — Mental Health Services Act (Prop 63 successor)**
California's dedicated mental health funding stream generates approximately $3.5B statewide annually. LA County's MHSA allocation: ~$400M/year. ACT teams for the chronically homeless unsheltered are among the highest-priority eligible expenditures under MHSA's Full Service Partnership (FSP) category. The MDI field architecture is a direct, eligible draw on this fund.

**3. SAMHSA Federal ACT Grants**
The federal Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration funds ACT implementation through competitive grants ($500K-$2M per program). California has historically been a strong SAMHSA ACT grantee. The MDI Stewardship Authority's role as a coordinating entity strengthens grant competitiveness by consolidating fragmented program applications.

**4. Measure Alpha and CARE Court Implementation**
- Measure Alpha ($843M county budget): covers residential infrastructure and a portion of field architecture operating costs under "Outcome Based Allocation."
- CARE Court Implementation (AB 1976, $14.7M appropriated statewide): the MDI Stewardship Authority's function as a CARE Court petitioning entity qualifies as an eligible implementation cost.

**Net Public Cost After Medi-Cal Billing Offset**:

| Cost Item | Annual Gross | Medi-Cal Offset | Net Public Cost |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Full ACT workforce (130 teams × ~$600K/team) | ~$78M | ~$75-85M | ~$0-5M |
| Registry/documentation teams (~300 FTE) | ~$18M | — | ~$18M |
| Pod Stewards + ground floor clinical | ~$24M | ~Partial Medi-Cal | ~$15M |
| **Total field + residential workforce** | **~$120M** | **~$75-85M** | **~$35-45M net** |

The net public cost of ~$35-45M annually for the full 40,000-person system falls well within the combined Measure Alpha and MHSA allocation available to LA County — before the Efficiency Surplus ($82M/tower × 20 towers = $1.64B annually at full network scale) begins flowing.

The workforce is not a cost problem. It is a **deficit-neutral employment pipeline** once Medi-Cal billing is accounted for.
