# 11_COMPARATIVE_MODELS: Evidence Base and International Precedents
## Material Dignity Infrastructure — What Has Been Proven to Work at Scale
*Feeds: The literature review and evidence base sections of the manuscript.*

---

### Overview

The MDI framework does not derive its architecture from theory. Every major design decision maps to a demonstrated precedent from a metropolitan, national, or clinical case study. This file catalogs the key comparative models, their measurable outcomes, and the specific MDI components they validate.

---

### Houston: "The Way Home" (2011-2024)

**Model Type**: Housing First, coordinated metropolitan field architecture.
**Scale**: Harris County, Texas — approximately 10,000+ individuals engaged over 13 years.

**Key Mechanisms**:
- Real-time by-name HMIS registry integrating 100+ partner agencies.
- Encampment-specific "Housing Surge Events" — coordinated placement offers at targeted sites.
- Clearance executed only after housing placement confirmed or actively in process.
- 90-day post-placement follow-up standard.

**Measured Outcomes**:
- 60% reduction in overall homelessness (2011-2020).
- Wheeler Encampment (2018): 58% placed in permanent supportive housing within the clearance event; 15% entered active navigation.
- Chartres Encampment (2019): 70% housing placement rate.
- 90% two-year housing retention system-wide.
- 127 encampments fully resolved (2021-2023); 75% of engaged individuals accepted housing navigation.

**MDI Validation**: Justifies the by-name HMIS registry as the operational backbone of the Field Architecture. Validates pre-staged unit matching as the mechanism for achieving 58-70% acceptance rates even in long-term encampment populations. Establishes the "leaf blower constraint" — clearance without housing = redistribution.

### Finland: National Housing First Program (2008-2022)

**Model Type**: National Housing First with permanent supported housing as the starting condition, not the reward.
**Scale**: National; concentrated in Helsinki.

**Key Mechanisms**:
- Conversion of legacy congregate shelters into permanent, scatter-site, and supported housing units.
- Y-Foundation (Y-Säätiö) as non-profit institutional landlord — buying, building, and leasing at scale.
- Multi-stakeholder collaboration: national government + municipalities + NGOs.
- Housing precedes all treatment conditions. No sobriety, employment, or behavioral requirements for entry.

**Measured Outcomes**:
- 68% reduction in long-term homelessness (2008-2022).
- Helsinki: dramatic reduction in emergency shelter beds; corresponding increase in permanent supported housing units.
- National commitment to complete eradication of homelessness by 2027.
- Cost-effectiveness documented: savings in emergency health, law enforcement, and judicial costs exceed program costs.

**MDI Validation**: Validates the governing principle ("Housing is the clinical precondition, not the reward"). Validates the non-profit institutional landlord model (Y-Foundation analog = MDI non-profit co-location partners). Validates scatter-site + supported housing as the two-track architecture matching MDI's ALMU + field architecture structure.

### The French Gardien Model

**Model Type**: On-site residential caretaker in French social housing (*Habitation à Loyer Modéré*, HLM).
**Scale**: National; thousands of high-rise social housing complexes.

**Key Mechanisms**:
- Live-in *Gardien* with ground-floor residential *loge*.
- Functions as social broker, maintenance coordinator, and informal conflict mediator.
- Intimate familiarity with individual residents — recognizes behavioral baseline and deviation.
- Bridges anonymous institutional management and daily human realities.

**Research Basis**: Harvard Graduate School of Design; TU Eindhoven housing research; Metropolitics.eu. Documents that social cohesion in large-scale HLM complexes correlates with Gardien engagement strength — buildings with reduced or consolidated Gardien presence show measurable social fragmentation.

**MDI Validation**: Justifies the Pod Steward model. Specifically validates the lived-in presence requirement, the social broker function, and the distinction between a security guard (enforcement) and a Gardien (recognition and relationship). Validates the 1:150 staffing ratio as the working scale for effective social cohesion maintenance.

### EmPATH Unit Model (Emergency Psychiatric Assessment, Treatment, and Healing)

**Model Type**: Non-coercive psychiatric emergency unit design.
**Scale**: Urban emergency departments across the United States.

**Key Mechanisms**:
- Open-floor-plan unit with recliner chairs rather than institutional beds — residential aesthetic.
- Rapid stabilization emphasis; avoids unnecessary hospitalization.
- Calming atmosphere; de-escalation as environmental design rather than pharmacological first response.

**Measured Outcomes**: Significant reductions in psychiatric boarding times and involuntary commitment rates compared to standard ED psychiatric intake. Documented reduction in use-of-force incidents.

**MDI Validation**: Justifies Zone A (Sally Port) and Zone B (Triage) ground floor design philosophy. Specifically validates the anti-institutional aesthetic requirement — warm materials, no correctional hardware — and the non-coercive de-escalation approach as clinically superior to security-first intake.

### CIT: Crisis Intervention Team (Memphis Model)

**Model Type**: Specialized law enforcement co-responder model.
**Scale**: Adopted in hundreds of US municipalities.

**Key Mechanisms**:
- 40-hour specialized training for law enforcement officers.
- Co-responder pairing: CIT officer + embedded mental health clinician.
- Psychiatric diversion: routing individuals to designated psychiatric emergency facilities rather than jail.
- Verbal de-escalation as primary tool.

**Measured Outcomes**:
- 28-58% reduction in use-of-force incidents (documented across multiple municipal studies).
- ~70% psychiatric diversion rate for eligible cases.

**Limitation**: Works sequentially (one individual at a time). Cannot manage simultaneous multi-actor escalation events. Kinetic separation (Sally Port) is the architectural equivalent enabling single-actor CIT de-escalation at MDI.

**MDI Validation**: Justifies dual-role crisis node staffing (CIT clinician + peer specialist) in Zone A/B. Validates the co-responder model. Establishes why multi-actor escalation requires architectural separation before clinical de-escalation can function.

### Crosstown Concourse (Memphis, USA)

**Model Type**: Adaptive reuse vertical urban village.
**Scale**: Former 1.5 million sq ft Sears warehouse converted to mixed-use vertical community.

**Key Mechanisms**:
- Housing combined with non-profit offices, education, arts, retail in a single vertical ecosystem.
- Mixed-use integration creates organic daily encounter across demographic groups.
- Ground-floor activation connects the building to the surrounding neighborhood.

**MDI Validation**: Validates the 4:1 civilian-to-resident integration ratio and the non-profit co-location model as architecture — not just policy. Demonstrates that stigma reduction of stabilization housing correlates with visible mixed-use activation and community permeability.

### Le Corbusier's Unité d'Habitation (Marseille, 1952)

**Model Type**: Early vertical self-contained community experiment.
**Scale**: 337 apartments, 1,600 residents, 17 stories.

**Key Mechanisms**: Internal "shopping street" at mid-building level, rooftop gymnasium and nursery, communal infrastructure embedded in residential fabric.

**Historical Significance**: Established the conceptual precedent that vertical density does not preclude community formation when common infrastructure is deliberately embedded rather than appended. The "streets in the air" concept prefigures the MDI pod-level sky garden and common kitchen placement logic.

**MDI Validation**: Historical architectural predecessor to the Dunbar Pod sky-garden and circulation loop design.
