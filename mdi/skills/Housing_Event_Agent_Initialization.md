## SECTION 1: WHAT IT IS NOT

Not a housing program. Not a shelter. Not a housing placement. Not a campaign.

---

## SECTION 2: DEFINITION

A Housing Event is a publicly visible, continuously operating, self-governing civic process in which a city's chronic street homelessness population is substantially housed through a purpose-built infrastructure system — in real time, transparently, in a specific geographic location, observed and verified by the public as it happens.

The word event is precise. In physics, an event is a point in spacetime: it has a location and a moment of occurrence. It is observable. It is measurable. A Housing Event is homelessness resolution happening — not being planned, not being funded — happening, continuously, at a named address, in a named city, visible on public screens in real time.

---

## SECTION 3: WHERE

A converted commercial skyscraper in the downtown core. MDI prototype: One California Plaza, Downtown Los Angeles.

Four conditions required simultaneously:
1. Distressed asset available at ~$120/sq ft (80% below peak CRE valuation)
2. Scale sufficient for 2,000 units in Dunbar Pods of 150
3. Location within or adjacent to highest chronic street homelessness concentration
4. Public visibility — LCD display network broadcasting real-time occupancy to the street

## SECTION 4: WHEN

Three trigger conditions must be simultaneously met:
1. Tower acquired by MDI Stewardship Authority through sovereign receivership
2. By-name HMIS registry documents every individual in target zone across minimum 6-month pre-operational deployment
3. Five MHRC-licensed Phase Zero clusters operational before first tower unit is offered

From activation: runs without pause. No closure for holidays, budget cycles, or political transitions.

Replication threshold: all eight verification metrics pass at One California Plaza before second tower activates.

---

## SECTION 5: WHY "EVENT"

Strategic: "Program" implies bureaucracy. "Initiative" implies politics. "Campaign" implies temporariness. "Event" is neutral, observable, present-tense.

Precise: A Housing Event is genuinely a different category. It is a process in which a population and a city co-produce a transformation — observable in real time, governed by residents, verified by eight independent metrics, replicable at any city containing a distressed commercial tower and a chronic street population.

The LCD display network is the event's public clock. It tells the city: this is happening. This many people slept inside last night. This many pods are at 85 percent capacity. The city watches itself solve its own problem. That watching is part of the solution.

## SECTION 6: FIVE SIMULTANEOUS PROCESSES

Process 1 — Field Architecture: ACT teams maintain sustained presence. Pre-matched warm offer at moment of willingness: named room, stored cart, kenneled pet, keycard. Offer does not expire.

Process 2 — Legal Lever: Court-supervised clinical pathway for anosognosic population only. Three existing California instruments: CARE Court, AOT, LPS Conservatorship. Anti-Detention Covenant guarantees unrestricted egress for all voluntary residents at all times.

Process 3 — Ground Floor Commons: Open 24/7/365. No intake required. No behavioral test. Food, hygiene, veterinary care, device charging, wound care, legal aid, peer support.

Process 4 — Riparian Environmental Enforcement: FLIR thermal drone mapping locates hidden encampments. Clean Water Act proceedings provide legal authority for abatement. Every abatement paired with simultaneous warm offer.

Process 5 — Ontological Permanence: Dunbar Pod structure, STC 65 acoustic sanctuary, Pod Steward, pod common kitchen, biophilic nodes, Digital Access Center rebuild neurological conditions for selfhood destroyed by street life.

## SECTION 7: EIGHT VERIFICATION METRICS

All eight must pass before replication. Binary gate — no partial success.

1. 70-80% encampment reduction in target zone within 24 months
2. ≥85% continuous housing retention at 24 months
3. ≥$40,000/resident/year emergency service cost reduction
4. ≥55% voluntary acceptance rate (Pipeline B)
5. ≥15% of Pipeline C housed via compelled pathways

7. ≥70% Phase Zero clearance at 60-90 days
8. Zero backlog exceeding 15 days for Phase Zero entry

General public: A Housing Event is a continuously operating, publicly visible process in which a city's distressed commercial skyscrapers are converted into self-governing stabilization infrastructure — and chronic street homelessness is reduced in real time, verified by data, watched by the city on public screens.

Policy/technical: A five-process parallel operational system — field architecture, legal lever, ground floor commons, environmental enforcement, and ontological permanence — running simultaneously from tower activation, verified against eight binary metrics, governed by a TVA-modeled Stewardship Authority, funded through a four-layer stack anchored in Medi-Cal sub-acute billing.

Responding to free-reign criticism: The Stewardship Contract governs tenure. The Rapid Transition Protocol transfers — not evicts — residents whose behavior creates danger. No resident becomes homeless through behavioral crisis.

Responding to crazy-dream criticism: A Housing Event is a falsifiable engineering specification. Eight metrics. One prototype. Measured outcomes only. Dreams are not falsifiable. Engineering specifications are.
