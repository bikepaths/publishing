# Chapter 11 Atomic Facts

## Section 1: Traditional Construction Limitations
- Traditional construction of new affordable housing requires five or more years for zoning approvals, environmental reviews, and financing negotiations.
- Raw land acquisition in downtown Los Angeles brings the cost of newly constructed units to over six hundred thousand dollars per unit.

## Section 2: Commercial Real Estate Defaults and Acquisition
- Commercial real estate high-rises lost corporate tenants between 2020 and 2025.
- One California Plaza in downtown Los Angeles defaulted on a three-hundred-million-dollar loan and entered bank receivership.
- The market price of One California Plaza collapsed to approximately one hundred and twenty dollars per square foot.
- The MDI Stewardship Authority operates under a TVA-modeled civic charter with state police power authority.
- The MDI Stewardship Authority can acquire defaulted properties at floor prices through receivership acquisitions.
- The acquisition of One California Plaza, consisting of one million square feet, costs one hundred and twenty million dollars.


## Section 3: One California Plaza Specifications and Conversion Timeline
- One California Plaza is located at 300 South Grand Avenue in the Financial District of Los Angeles, adjacent to Skid Row.
- The building has a floor plate of one million square feet.
- Adaptive reuse strips the interior to concrete pillars and the structural skeleton without demolition.
- Conversions upgrade electrical distribution, plumbing risers, and mechanical systems for residential density.
- Existing HVAC systems, elevator banks, and structural systems are maintained.
- Adaptive reuse conversions target a residential occupancy certificate within twelve months of acquisition.
- The MDI tower model requires a minimum ceiling clearance of nine feet to support the raised utility run plenum.
- The building floor plate must support a live load of eighty pounds per square foot to permit modular ALMU installation without structural reinforcement.


## Section 4: ALMU Interior Buildout and Dunbar Pod Division
- The interior buildout constructs two thousand Autonomous Living Module Units, or ALMUs.
- Each ALMU contains one hundred and fifty net square feet of private space.
- ALMU interiors use matte birch plywood paneling and stone-texture wall surfaces.
- The gross residential footprint per unit is four hundred square feet.
- Two hundred and fifty square feet per unit is allocated to corridors, pod biophilic nodes, common kitchens, elevator lobbies, and mechanical chases.
- The tower contains twenty-eight elevator banks divided into thirteen independent loops, with one loop per Dunbar Pod.


## Section 5: Capital Expenditures and Resource Infrastructure
- Total prototype capital expenditure is one hundred and ninety-five million dollars.
- The expenditure is divided between one hundred and twenty million dollars for building acquisition, fifty million dollars for two thousand residential modules costing twenty-five thousand dollars per unit, and twenty-five million dollars for physical plant upgrades including Sound Transmission Class sixty-five acoustic upgrades, HVAC modifications, cistern installation, and backup power.
- The backup generator system is a hydrogen or dual-fuel system with a ninety-six-hour operational mandate.
- The potable water cistern holds five hundred and sixty thousand gallons across three basement levels.
- The total capital investment is recoverable within twenty-nine months of full tower activation.
