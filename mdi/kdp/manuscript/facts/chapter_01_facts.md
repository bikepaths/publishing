# Chapter 1 Atomic Facts

## Section 1: The Subterranean Construction
- In May 2026, a video recorded a subterranean living space beneath a Los Angeles bridge within the 110 freeway support pillars.
- The video documented lighting grids drawing municipal electricity from a city junction box two blocks away.
- The electricity powered lights and small appliances across a span of dozens of blocks.
- The living spaces contained dozens of individual rooms built with scavenged industrial materials.
- The layout of these rooms was complex.

## Section 2: Construction Dynamics
- The subterranean quarters required months of construction.
- The builders possessed mechanical skills to install the electrical grid under the freeway.
- The structure used concrete support pillars to block winter rain and deflect winds.
- The location was chosen to remain invisible to police sweeps in open public spaces.
- City officials did not examine the freeway support pillar location.

## Section 3: State Spending and Demographics
- Starting in 2019, California spent twenty-four billion dollars on crisis management.
- The funds were distributed through dozens of agencies and regional offices.
- No testing was done to verify if the spending sequence matched the biological requirements of the unhoused population.
- In 2025, the Los Angeles Homeless Services Authority counted more than seventy-five thousand unhoused individuals in Greater Los Angeles County.
- Fifty-eight percent of the counted population slept outside on any given night.
- Within Los Angeles County, approximately seventy thousand people lived on the streets.
- The unhoused population grew throughout the five-year funding cycle.

## Section 4: Physical Infrastructure
- Politicians and media organizations use the word "encampment" to describe these locations.
- The May 2026 video showed permanent residential infrastructure rather than temporary tents.
- The infrastructure was built using salvaged materials.
- The builders spent weeks installing electrical wiring to prevent fires.
- The layout of the structures was planned for seasonal weather patterns.

## Section 5: Comparative Policy Outcomes
- Finland's Housing First programs achieve an eighty to ninety percent housing retention rate over two years.
- The United States achieves below forty percent housing retention over two years for the same type of program.
- Finland delivers nutritional support, healthcare access, and social stabilization before placing individuals in permanent housing.
- The United States places individuals in apartments during acute physiological crises.
- A person unable to maintain life on the street is unable to maintain a housing lease.
