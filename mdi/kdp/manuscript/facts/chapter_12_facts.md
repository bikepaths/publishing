# Chapter 12 Atomic Facts

## Section 1: The Three Survival Anchors
- Existing shelters require individuals to surrender three primary survival anchors consisting of animal companions, intimate partners, and accumulated possessions.
- Animal companions provide emotional regulation and physical warmth to unhoused individuals.
- Intimate partners reduce risks of violence, theft, and exposure mortality on the street.
- Accumulated possessions represent the material capital of unhoused individuals.

## Section 2: The Kennel Infrastructure
- The MDI tower features a professionally managed kennel facility on the second floor.
- Each animal companion occupies an individual, climate-controlled run.
- The kennel is sound-dampened and odor-managed.
- Residents have free access to the kennel from six in the morning until ten at night.
- An on-site non-profit veterinary partnership provides medical care, vaccinations, behavioral assessments, and spay-neuter services.

## Section 3: Partners and Possessions Integration
- Partners are assigned two adjacent ALMUs within the same Dunbar Pod, matched through the Homeless Management Information System database.
- Adjacent units have separate locked doors side by side.
- Possessions are stored in individual ground-floor bays.
- Storage bays are photographed, GPS-tagged, and accessed via personal RFID cards.
- Staff members cannot access storage bays without the resident's consent.


## Section 4: ALMU Interior Architecture and Amenities
- The ALMU contains one hundred and fifty net square feet of private space.
- The ALMU features a solid-core door with a Sound Transmission Class sixty-five acoustic frame and threshold seal.
- The MDI modular unit features structural isolation hangers that decouple the interior framing from the building core, achieving a Sound Transmission Class of sixty-five.
- A Murphy wall-bed folds flat to return approximately thirty-five square feet of floor space.
- A wall-mounted fold-down desk features seated and standing configurations.
- Kitchen amenities include a one-point-seven cubic foot mini-refrigerator, an induction cooktop, and a microwave alcove.
- Gas cooking is prohibited in the ALMU.
- The ALMU wardrobe column is twelve inches deep and twenty-four inches wide.
- The private wet bath requires a twenty-eight to thirty-two square foot footprint, to establish full sanitary sovereignty, containing a toilet, wall-mounted sink, showerhead, and drain-sloped floor.
- Water temperature is controlled by the resident and pre-heated via solar thermal systems.
- There are no communal hygiene facilities in the building.


## Section 5: Circadian Lighting and Glazing
- Perimeter ALMUs feature full-height commercial glazing with floor-to-ceiling sky views.
- Interior units feature programmable full-spectrum circadian LED ceiling panels that simulate daylight cycles over a twenty-four-hour period.
- Circadian LED lighting reduces seasonal affective disorder and circadian sleep disruption.
