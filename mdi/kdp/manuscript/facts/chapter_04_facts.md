# Chapter 4 Atomic Facts

## Section 1: Clearance Operations and Documentation
- A sweep operation deploys sanitation crews, police units, and dumpsters to clear encampments.
- Authorities arrive at encampments between four and six in the morning.
- Workers throw tents, personal property, and medications into garbage trucks.
- Bulldozers push structural debris into steel dumpsters.
- The operation concludes within hours.
- City databases log the completion of the clearance.
- No records track where the displaced individuals go after the clearance.

## Section 2: Health Impact and Social Networks
- Medical literature documents that dispersing encampments without housing increases disease, accelerates chronic conditions, and shortens life expectancy.
- Encampment clearances destroy residents' survival networks.
- Clearance eliminates shared information about which emergency rooms ignore police, food lines, and neighbors who administer first aid.
- Rebuilding survival networks after forced displacement takes weeks.

## Section 3: Dispersal and Drug Overdose Mortality
- Encampment communities monitor for overdoses, administer naloxone, and call emergency services.
- Dispersal pushes drug users into isolated locations where they use drugs alone.
- Overdosing alone without witnesses leads to fatalities.
- Quick response times within established camps prevent fatalities during overdoses.
- Sweeps remove the community that intervenes in overdoses.

## Section 4: D.C. Federal Clearance Experiment
- Between 2025 and 2026, federal authorities executed more than one hundred clearance operations in Washington, D.C.
- The operations proceeded without coordination with local outreach organizations and without pre-arranged housing placements.
- The displaced population migrated into Maryland and northern Virginia.
- The migrating population carried unresolved medical and psychiatric conditions.
- New jurisdictions absorbed the associated costs.
- During the clearance period, the Washington, D.C. point-in-time count recorded a four percent increase in the overall unhoused population.

## Section 5: Pre-Matched Housing Outcomes
- Houston and Dallas pre-matched individuals to specific units before clearing physical sites.
- In Houston and Dallas, physical clearance followed housing placements.
- Houston achieved a sixty percent reduction in its unsheltered population.
- Dallas achieved a twenty-four percent reduction in its unsheltered population using a pre-matched model.
- Both cities used encampments as contact points for outreach.
