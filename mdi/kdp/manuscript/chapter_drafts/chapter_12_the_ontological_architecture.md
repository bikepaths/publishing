A physical building only works if people actually agree to walk through the front door. The current shelter system builds massive warehouses and then enforces rules that keep the most vulnerable people trapped outside. When a city tells a woman she must abandon her dog to get a bed, she chooses the pavement. When a shelter separates a husband from his wife, they both refuse and remain in a tent together. The rules create the barrier. The system punishes human attachment.

We call these structural barriers the Three Ps: Pets, Partners, and Possessions. The MDI tower eliminates all three barriers simultaneously through direct physical design rather than policy waivers. A professionally managed kennel facility on the second floor of the tower provides individual climate-controlled runs for every animal companion, with unlimited resident access from early morning until late evening. Zero cost to the resident. An on-site veterinary partnership handles all medical care and vaccinations. The animal never leaves the building, and neither does its owner.

Partners present a different physical requirement. Two people who survived the street together do not share one room easily. Each person requires their own sovereign space to rebuild their nervous system. The pipeline assigns partners to two adjacent ALMUs within the same Dunbar Pod. Two locked doors, side by side. The couple lives within fifteen feet of each other but retains independent private spaces for the critical biological reset. The intake system matches them at the point of assignment through the housing management information system. They never separate.

Possessions require a different architectural solution. A person's shopping cart holds every physical object they own in the world, and threatening to throw it away destroys the trust necessary to enter the building. The pipeline provides GPS-tracked, photographed, and tagged secure storage on the ground floor with RFID card access available around the clock. The resident retains full control of every object they own. No staff member can touch their property without consent. The storage guarantee converts the offer from a demand for surrender into a rational proposition the person can accept without losing everything.

The walls of the ALMU itself perform the heaviest lifting in the biological recovery process. Engineers construct the units to a Sound Transmission Class rating of 65, which reduces ambient sound transfer to levels approaching a clinical quiet room. A person lying in bed hears near-total silence instead of police sirens and screaming. Because complete silence can disorient individuals habituated to outdoor environments, the ventilation system introduces low-amplitude biological white noise at frequencies resembling running water and wind. The person sleeps. The brain resets. The next chapter explains the automated machine that keeps the building running without turning it into an institution.
