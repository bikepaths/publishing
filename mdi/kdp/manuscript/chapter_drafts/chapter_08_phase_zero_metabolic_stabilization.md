The current housing system ignores a specific, documented biological sequence. When a city removes a person from the street and places them directly into a permanent apartment, it delivers a stable environment to a nervous system that is not yet capable of processing stability. The stress chemicals produced by years of continuous threat exposure do not vanish because the mailing address changes. The body remains on a hair trigger. Unfamiliar quiet registers as a threat. Enclosed spaces trigger panic in individuals habituated to open environments where escape routes are always visible. The resulting behavioral failures, the stripped copper wiring, the invited encampment contacts, the abandoned lease, are not moral failures. They are documented neurological outcomes of placing a person in a maintenance state before completing the repair sequence.

Phase Zero is the repair sequence. It operates as a mandatory clinical staging period between the street and the permanent unit, running for sixty to ninety days inside licensed Mental Health Rehabilitation Center facilities deployed as sub-sixteen-unit clusters within the Skid Row target zone. The sub-sixteen-unit cluster architecture is not administrative preference. It is a legal specification that targets the Institution for Mental Disease Exclusion, the federal rule that disqualifies Medi-Cal billing for psychiatric facilities above fifteen beds. Each cluster of fifteen beds files an independent licensure application with the California Department of Health Care Services under Form 1813 and bills Medi-Cal as a specialty mental health sub-acute facility at approximately fifty percent federal cost participation. A sixty-day stabilization episode generates approximately fifteen thousand dollars per enrolled resident, with roughly seven thousand five hundred dollars flowing from federal Medi-Cal matching funds. The cluster model funds the most clinically intensive phase of the pipeline under existing California law without requiring new legislation.

The clinical protocol inside Phase Zero targets four simultaneous biological systems in a specific sequence because the sequence determines whether the interventions succeed or compound each other's failures. Hours zero through four prioritize rehydration using World Health Organization electrolyte solutions rather than plain water. This distinction is medically critical: rapid water loading in severely dehydrated individuals with depleted sodium triggers hyponatremia, a cerebral edema condition that can be fatal. The rehydration protocol is therefore a life-safety specification, not a hospitality gesture. Hours four through twenty-four introduce nutritional support under Refeeding Syndrome protocol. Severely malnourished individuals face a life-threatening risk when carbohydrates are reintroduced rapidly, because the resulting electrolyte shifts produce cardiac arrhythmia. The cafeteria routes every individual with a Body Mass Index below sixteen or visible severe wasting into a gradual reintroduction sequence before open food access begins.

Hours twenty-four through seventy-two focus on wound care, infection treatment, and sleep initiation under acoustic isolation. The Sound Transmission Class 65 specification of the Phase Zero module is a therapeutic standard rather than a luxury specification. Auditory hypersensitivity is a documented symptom of methamphetamine-induced psychosis during post-acute withdrawal, and hypervigilance is a DSM-5 diagnostic criterion for Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder. Uncontrolled environmental noise during this window is a documented recovery barrier, not a minor discomfort. The acoustic enclosure is the clinical instrument. Following initial stabilization, the psychiatric assessment window opens between days twenty-four and seventy-two. Attempting psychiatric evaluation before this window produces false assessments because the individual's neurological baseline is still suppressed by acute dehydration, malnutrition, and drug withdrawal, not by their underlying psychiatric condition.

A person exits Phase Zero when they meet at least two of five measurable functional indicators across the sixty to ninety day stabilization window: sustained hygiene maintenance without staff prompting; six or more hours of uninterrupted sleep without pharmacological support; consistent orientation to person, place, and date across three consecutive days; participation in at least one structured group activity per day; and independent initiation of at least one daily living task per day. These indicators are not subjective impressions of clinical readiness. They are the measurement substrate for the eighth of the eight binary verification metrics that determine whether the prototype has succeeded. The person who exits Phase Zero meeting these indicators can hold a lease. The person who skips Phase Zero and receives a direct placement cannot. The biology must precede the key.
