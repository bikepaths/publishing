A functioning pipeline requires exact measurements. Once a city stops treating the street as a single massive problem, it can finally start sorting the population into actionable categories. We can break the entire crisis into four distinct physical pipelines. Each track demands a completely different mechanical intervention. We call these pipelines the Voluntary, the Engageable, the Calcified, and the Nomadic. Matching the person to the correct track determines whether the machine succeeds or fails.

Pipeline A contains the Voluntary population. These individuals recently lost their housing due to a sudden financial shock like a medical emergency or a lost job. They possess the mental hardware and the physical energy required to navigate normal society. They simply lack cash. The mechanical solution for this group requires rapid, direct financial support rather than complex medical interventions. Providing short-term rental assistance immediately moves them off the street. They require a checkbook rather than a doctor.

Pipeline B captures the Engageable population. These people suffer from treatable physical or mental barriers that block them from holding a standard job. They might fight an addiction or manage a moderate mental illness. Crucially, they recognize they need help and willingly accept clinical treatment. The mechanical solution here requires dedicated social workers and access to stable recovery programs. They need a quiet, safe room where they can reset their nervous system and receive medical care. They require a guided path.

Pipeline C represents the Calcified population. These individuals suffer from severe, untreated schizophrenia or end-stage drug addiction that destroys their ability to make rational choices. They cannot voluntarily engage with treatment because their biological hardware is actively failing. A person actively hallucinating on the pavement cannot fill out a housing application. The mechanical solution for this group requires compelled, state-ordered medical intervention. The state must step in and provide a secure, locked clinical environment to save their lives. The street functions as a death sentence for this group.

Pipeline D defines the Nomadic population. These individuals actively reject standard housing and clinical intervention because they prefer the absolute freedom of the underground city. They possess the mental capacity to make choices but refuse to participate in the rigid rules of normal society. They often run the electrical grids and organize the physical defenses of the large encampments. The city cannot force them into traditional apartments. The mechanical solution requires designated, heavily monitored industrial zones where they can camp legally without destroying public infrastructure. The city must contain them rather than cure them.

These four distinct tracks represent an unbreakable pipeline law. Forcing a working mother from Pipeline A into a locked medical facility wastes millions of dollars. Leaving a severely ill man from Pipeline C alone on the street guarantees his early death. The current system fails entirely because it shoves all four populations into the exact same broken shelter. A successful industrial infrastructure routes each individual into the specific mechanical track designed for their exact biological reality. The proper categorization saves the machine.
