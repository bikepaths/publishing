## 2. BOOK DESCRIPTION (Revised — implemented)

Twenty-four billion dollars spent in California since 2019. Forty-six thousand people still on the street in Los Angeles alone. The money is not the problem. The sequence is.

Cities place individuals into housing while their bodies remain in acute physiological crisis — sleep-destroyed, metabolically collapsed, neurologically compromised by years of untreated psychosis. The result is sub-40 percent housing retention. Finland runs the same Housing First program and achieves 80 to 90 percent retention. The difference is not the housing. It is what Finland does before the housing.

Building Material Dignity engineers that missing layer for Los Angeles — and builds the replicable model for every city on earth facing the same sequencing failure.

This is not a policy proposal. It is an industrial manual.

The blueprint converts vacant downtown skyscrapers — available at 80 percent below peak valuation following the commercial real estate collapse — into self-governing civic infrastructure. The ground floor operates 24 hours a day, 365 days a year: open to anyone, no conditions, no intake forms required. Showers. Food. Veterinary care for animal companions. Charging stations. Sleeping modules. Human dignity without a behavioral test to earn it.

Above the ground floor, the tower partitions into Dunbar Pods — independent neighborhoods of exactly 150 people, the hard biological limit of human trust. Each resident holds the only key to their own room. Each pod has a common kitchen, a garden, a Pod Steward who knows every resident by name. A cybernetic monitoring layer displays real-time occupancy across the network on public LCD screens, making the housing event visible to the city as it happens.

Five structurally distinct street populations receive five matched intervention pipelines. The economically displaced get rapid placement. The encamped but engageable get a specific, immediate offer that eliminates the three primary refusal barriers: forced separation from animal companions, partners, and possessions. The behaviorally calcified chronic receive 12 to 24 months of sustained outreach before a warm offer at the moment of willingness. Those whose neurology prevents them from perceiving their own danger receive court-supervised clinical intervention — not imprisonment, but the only path that reaches them. The voluntary nomadic receive harm reduction without any placement requirement. No legal instrument touches them.

The fiscal math generates an annual Efficiency Surplus of 33 to 82 million dollars per tower. Capital recovery occurs within 29 to 71 months. The entire field workforce is deficit-neutral once Medi-Cal billing is accounted for. The system hires preferentially from the population it serves.

Eight binary verification metrics define the Singular Prototype Threshold. Every metric must pass at One California Plaza before a single replication tower is acquired. The blueprint is falsifiable. If it fails, it fails on measured data — not political narrative.

The author engineered this framework from three decades of unsheltered lived experience on the streets and bike paths of America and the world. He did not study the problem from outside. He built the solution from inside it.

The window to act is narrowing. The distressed buildings are available now. The blueprint is finished.

## 3. AUTHOR BIOGRAPHY

**Long Version (implemented on Amazon author page):**

Charles J. DiBella is a Principal Systems Architect whose work sits at the intersection of urban economics, clinical systems design, and public infrastructure engineering. Since 1997 he has lived and traveled full-time by bicycle — logging countless coast-to-coast American journeys alongside extensive tours through Southeast Asia and Europe. This life, conducted at street level and across decades, provided the observational foundation for the Material Dignity Infrastructure.

DiBella's framework did not emerge from academic study of homelessness. It emerged from direct, sustained immersion in the physical realities of street life — the metabolic destruction, the sleep deprivation, the social isolation, the survival architecture that street communities build when every institutional system fails them. When economics training led him to recognize the commercial real estate collapse as a one-off civic opportunity inside a closing time window, the tower pipeline that had been forming in his observations over thirty years crystallized into an industrial specification.

If it fails, the data shows exactly where and why. If it succeeds, Los Angeles becomes the model for every city on earth facing the same sequencing failure.

DiBella blogs at bikepaths.org under the title Cycles of Change and produces companion audio architecture for the MDI framework through the site's podcast channel.
