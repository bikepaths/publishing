# EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: MATERIAL DIGNITY INFRASTRUCTURE (MDI)
## Prototype Demonstration: One California Plaza
**Date:** June 2026
**Confidentiality:** Institutional Distribution Only

### 1. INVESTMENT THESIS
Chronic urban displacement represents a structural architectural failure requiring systemic capitalization, rather than a moral deficit requiring philanthropic mitigation. Los Angeles currently absorbs two billion dollars annually in sunk operational costs responding to emergency biological decay. The Material Dignity Infrastructure (MDI) framework converts these catastrophic municipal liabilities into collateralized, cash-flowing real estate equity. 

We propose acquiring One California Plaza at deeply distressed valuations (sub-replacement cost) to establish the first fully functional MDI prototype. The facility will stabilize one thousand metabolically compromised individuals, generating fifty thousand dollars per capita in verified avoided municipal costs. Autonomous replication across global capital markets initiates immediately following the Day 365 performance audit. 

### 2. CAPITALIZATION ARCHITECTURE
Total required capitalization for the One California Plaza acquisition and stabilization phase stands at $220,000,000. This capital stack is explicitly bifurcated to isolate and absorb initial operational demonstration risk.

**Tranche A: Catalytic First-Loss Equity ($88,000,000 | 40%)**
Targeting mission-driven family offices and philanthropic endowments utilizing Program-Related Investments (PRIs). This tranche absorbs primary operational volatility during the initial thirty-six-month ramp period. It carries a deferred four percent target yield, functioning explicitly to de-risk the senior debt tranche and validate the underlying operational model.

**Tranche B: Senior Concessionary Debt ($132,000,000 | 60%)**
Targeting Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs) and mission-aligned commercial lenders. This tranche requires a standard eight percent yield. The loan is tightly secured against the underlying physical commercial real estate asset, which is acquired far below replacement cost, providing robust downside protection. 

*Note: The total capitalization includes a $10.56M capitalized interest reserve covering thirty-six months of debt service during facility stabilization.*

### 3. LEGAL AND GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE
The project utilizes a Tripartite Coalition structure, deliberately bypassing inefficient municipal procurement channels to ensure operational autonomy and execution velocity.

*   **Delaware Statutory Trust (DST):** Holds absolute title to the physical real estate assets, shielding the property from localized operational liabilities and political interference.
*   **California Public Benefit Corporation (PBC):** Executes all daily facility operations, relational stabilization protocols, and clinical service coordination.

### 4. REVENUE MECHANICS
Operational sustainability relies entirely upon sovereign state funding, completely bypassing volatile municipal grant cycles. The PBC will directly bill managed care plans under the California Advancing and Innovating Medi-Cal (CalAIM) Community Supports framework. 

Core sustainable revenue streams include:
*   Housing Transition Navigation Services (HCPCS: H0043 U6)
*   Housing Deposits (HCPCS: H0044 U2)
*   Housing Tenancy and Sustaining Services (HCPCS: T2050 U6)
*   Recuperative Care (Medical Respite) (HCPCS: T2033 U6)

### 5. STRATEGIC CONCLUSION
The One California Plaza prototype mathematically eliminates the first-mover demonstration risk currently paralyzing institutional capital deployment. By executing this single fully funded prototype, the MDI framework establishes the audited financial baselines required to unlock limitless institutional liquidity for solving chronic urban displacement globally.
