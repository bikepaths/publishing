# Conceptual Bibliography

This manual relies on the hard mechanics of biology, neurology, and physical engineering rather than political theory. The following foundational concepts and texts provide the structural proof for the pipelines engineered in this blueprint.

### I. Neurological Limits and Compelled Care
**Anosognosia (The Broken Brain)**
The physical inability of a diseased brain to perceive its own illness. This structural failure demands the use of the Legal Lever.
*   **Amador, Xavier (2000).** *I Am Not Sick, I Don't Need Help! How to Help Someone Accept Treatment.* Vida Press. (Foundational text on recognizing and navigating anosognosia).

**Dunbar’s Number (Social Scaling Limits)**
The hard biological limit on the number of stable social relationships a human brain can track (approximately 150).
*   **Dunbar, R. I. M. (1992).** *Neocortex size as a constraint on group size in primates.* Journal of Human Evolution, 22(6), 469–493. (The mathematical baseline for the 150-person Dunbar Pod architecture).

### II. Physiological Weathering and Metabolic Stabilization
**Allostatic Load and Biological Weathering**
The physiological wear and tear on the human body caused by chronic stress, sleep deprivation, and street-level exposure.
*   **McEwen, B. S. (1998).** *Stress, adaptation, and disease: Allostasis and allostatic load.* Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences, 840, 33–44. (The baseline requirement for the Phase Zero metabolic airlock).

**Sensory Gating and Acoustic Isolation**
The necessity of absolute acoustic and physical boundaries for the central nervous system to exit a state of hyper-arousal.
*   **Acoustic Society of America.** Standards for Sound Transmission Class (STC). (The engineering standard defining the STC 65 soundproofing requirement for Phase Zero recovery units).

### III. Infrastructure and Economic Architecture
**The Limits of Housing First**
Critiques of the legacy protocol's failure to sequence physiological recovery prior to permanent placement.
*   **UCSF Benioff Homelessness and Housing Initiative (2023).** *Toward a New Understanding: The California Statewide Study of People Experiencing Homelessness.* (Demonstrating the raw statistical failure rates of current street-to-home retention).

**Compelled Care Frameworks**
The legal architecture required to execute physical recovery when the individual lacks neurological capacity.
*   **California CARE Court Legislation (SB 1338).** (The legislative tool that functions as the Legal Lever, allowing judges to mandate clinical stabilization).
