# Chapter 10: The Municipal Quarantine

In the 1800s, a disease called cholera killed thousands of people in London and New York. The sickness spread through dirty water and raw waste on the streets. City leaders did not stop to argue about whether the sick people were good or bad. They did not spend years deciding who deserved help. Instead, they built the sewer grid. The cities spent huge amounts of money to build underground pipes that carried the waste away because the alternative was the total collapse of the city.

This history provides the exact blueprint for our project. A city builds public utilities like sewers strictly to keep itself alive. Sanitation pipes do not exist because the leaders feel sympathy for the poor. They exist because raw waste and disease destroy the value of buildings and explode hospital bills for everyone. The cost of a disease outbreak is much higher than the cost of the pipes. For this reason, the sewer is an act of physical self-preservation for the whole city.

The tents on our streets today create the exact same problem for the public budget. The unmanaged street population creates public health problems and damages the value of nearby businesses. Right now, the old system responds with police sweeps that only move the problem around. This reaction burns millions of dollars but solves nothing since it never fixes the root cause. The city is paying for a machine that manages the mess rather than a system that contains it. We are repeating the mistake made before they built the first sewers.

The Material Dignity Infrastructure acts as a Metabolic Gateway. It treats our towers as a public utility built to contain the physical friction of the street. This argument does not need to talk about right or wrong. Voters already pay for power lines and water pipes without asking if the users have perfect behavior. They pay for these systems because the alternative would ruin their own lives and property. Our project uses this exact same logic. The city builds the towers to protect itself from the physical crisis.
