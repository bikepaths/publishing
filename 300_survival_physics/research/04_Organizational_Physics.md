# 04_ORGANIZATIONAL_PHYSICS: Dunbar-Scale Governance and Shift Operations

## Dunbar-Scale Organizational Logic

The design of the pilot tower workforce rests on a neurological constraint: the human cognitive capacity for stable social relationships is bounded by Dunbar's number of approximately 150 individuals. By restricting the total pilot workforce to 151.1 Full-Time Equivalents, the administrative architecture prevents the formation of anonymous bureaucracies. Every worker maintains direct cognitive recognition of every colleague. This flat relational landscape eliminates middle-management layers that historically capture municipal service contracts and block operational agility.

Relational trust is the primary currency of high-acuity encampment engagement and tower stabilization. When a team expands beyond the Dunbar limit, informal accountability systems collapse and require replacement by formal compliance mechanisms. These rigid mechanisms degrade the flexibility required for clinical de-escalation. By locking the staff size to a Dunbar unit, the pilot tower preserves organic peer-to-peer accountability and reinforces the unified mission of metropolitan survival.

## Live-in Pod Stewardship Model

The 13 residential pods require continuous leadership without the friction of hourly shift rotations. The MDI model resolves this through the live-in Pod Steward role. One steward resides permanently in each pod, occupying a designated unit adjacent to the biophilic alcove.

Pod Stewards do not perform clinical interventions or security enforcement. They act as community anchors who facilitate weekly pod councils and monitor baseline residential stability. Because they share the immediate somatic environment, they detect micro-stressors and unit-level anomalies before they escalate into clinical crises. Their compensation includes sovereign residency and full dining access, which reduces payroll strain while embedding leadership directly into the vertical architecture.

## Volunteer Intern Integration

Stabilizing operational costs requires mitigating the high turnover rates common in entry-level human services. The pilot tower integrates the Socio-Educational Residency Program to deploy volunteer interns under twelve-month commitments. This program targets roles that do not require clinical licenses: hygiene attendants, storage attendants, and rest chamber stewards.

Interns receive specific incentives in exchange for one year of service:
1. Sovereign residency in designated staff units.
2. Full dining hall access.
3. Post-service educational credits or professional certification.

By trading material assets for labor, the Stewardship Authority isolates the operating budget from market labor fluctuations. This internship pipeline creates a steady flow of trained operators who understand the somatic system, forming a recruitment pool for permanent staff roles.

## Operational Unity and Sovereign Labor Charter

To prevent administrative drift, the Stewardship Authority operates under a Sovereign Labor Charter. This charter exempts the pilot tower from standard municipal civil service hiring processes, allowing the authority to hire peer support specialists with lived experience based on trust metrics rather than academic credentials.

Administrative overhead remains capped through a lean management structure:
- Management Operations: 6.0 Full-Time Equivalents. Handles accounting, clinical human resource coordination, and executive oversight.
- Billing Integrity Staff: 3.6 Full-Time Equivalents. Manages real-time Medi-Cal billing to minimize leakage.

This specialized administration focuses entirely on protecting the somatic environment and maximizing insurance capture, ensuring the organization remains unified toward the singular mission of metropolitan survival.
