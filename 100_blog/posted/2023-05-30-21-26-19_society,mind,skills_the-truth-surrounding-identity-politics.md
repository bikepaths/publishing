Philosophers argue about the meaning of truth. While some define truth as objective facts that exist without our opinions, others see it as personal. Under one view, physical facts like the shape of the earth remain constant regardless of what we believe. In contrast, two friends watching a movie might disagree on its value. By looking at these views, we find that truth can also change based on cultural settings.

In public debates, identity politics centers on groups defined by race, gender, and sexual orientation. Shared stories of daily struggles explain these group lives. Although these stories provide deep meaning, they cannot represent the experiences of every person in the group. When individuals live different lives, one story does not write the rules for all.

To build fair public policies, lawmakers need objective facts. Hard statistics and public records show wide trends that affect the whole nation. By listening to individual accounts, we discover the real human struggles that people face under these laws every single day. Through this balance, we make our discussions both useful and kind.
