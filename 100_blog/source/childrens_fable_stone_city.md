# The Stone City Without Bottom

The great builders made a city out of white stone. They built tall towers reaching toward clouds. They built thick walls keeping out wind. 

Inside the walls lived strong workers who polished stones daily. Outside the walls lived tired wanderers who lacked strength to lift stones. 

"Do not let them inside," the master builder said. "They cannot work. They will only eat our bread. They will only occupy our space."

So the tired wanderers remained outside. When cold winds blew, the wanderers dug holes beneath the great white walls seeking shelter from frost. They dug with bare hands. They scooped away dirt holding the heavy stones up. 

Inside, the strong workers felt safe. They baked warm bread. They polished white stones until everything gleamed. They never looked down at the dirt.

More tired wanderers arrived outside. More cold winds blew. The wanderers dug deeper holes beneath the walls. They hollowed out the earth beneath the magnificent white city.

One night, the master builder felt the ground shake. He looked out from his tallest tower. He saw no armies attacking. He saw no storms approaching. He only saw tired wanderers huddled inside deep holes dug beneath his perfect walls.

"Send guards to chase them away," the master builder ordered. 

Guards marched toward the walls carrying heavy spears. But when they reached the walls, they stopped. The walls were leaning outward.

The dirt holding the city up was gone. The tired wanderers had scraped it all away seeking warmth. 

The master builder shouted commands from his tall tower. He commanded the walls to stand straight. He commanded the stones to hold tight. 

Stones do not listen to commands. Stones only listen to gravity.

The great white walls tilted outward. Then they fell. They crushed the deep holes where the tired wanderers huddled. They crushed the warm ovens where the strong workers baked bread. The tall tower cracked down the middle.

When morning came, no white city remained. Only broken stones covered the dirt.
