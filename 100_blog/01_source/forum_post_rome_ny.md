Title: 25-Year Global Solo Veteran Seeking Snail-Paced, Self-Supported Company near Rome, NY
Description: A local call for a self-reliant riding partner willing to match a slow pace along the Erie Canal.
Tags: adventure, bicycle-touring, survival, resilience, health, community

Twenty-five years of pedaling a bicycle across the world alone taught me real self-reliance, and I chose to travel without a support car or a crew because I wanted complete freedom on the road. But turning seventy brings a long riding break and some extra weight. My joints are finally giving out, so I have to ride a recumbent bicycle just to keep my body moving, and ongoing medical needs restrict my travel to short local loops around the Erie Canal starting out of Rome, New York. My physical ability to turn the pedals is still completely fine, but riding alone gets boring and my motivation drops without a partner. Healthy adults and younger riders pass me immediately on the trail, and that huge difference in speed guarantees I end up riding alone even when I want company. A good riding partner just needs to ignore the urge to go fast and forget about hitting a big daily mileage. We can connect for a ride to trade patience for knowledge, just logging short and slow miles together while I pass on everything I learned about surviving on the road. This slow pace keeps our aging bodies safe while getting us back outside where we belong.
