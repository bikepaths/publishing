# Specification Plan: Relational Integration Gaps

## 1. Strategic Baseline
RDI Working Paper 4 successfully names and theoretically maps two integration gaps (The Relational Intake Protocol and the Social Network Transition Protocol). It establishes *why* they are necessary. The next operational phase requires moving from theoretical justification to procedural engineering. We must write the manuals.

This plan details the granular execution of those specifications.

---

## 2. Gap 1: Relational Intake Protocol (Neuroception Architecture)
**Objective:** Translate Polyvagal Theory (Porges) into a step-by-step outreach and intake operating procedure.

**Phase A: Sequence Mapping**
*   **Action:** Map the standard MDI "Warm Offer" timeline from initial street contact to first night of occupancy.
*   **Deliverable:** A chronological flowchart isolating every human interaction point during intake.

**Phase B: Neuroception Checklisting**
*   **Action:** Engineer a binary compliance checklist for outreach workers based on bottom-up regulation mechanics.
*   **Elements to Specify:**
    *   *The "Who" Constraint:* Algorithmic routing to ensure the outreach worker with highest historical contact frequency executes the offer.
    *   *The "What" Scripting:* Mandated inclusion of specific historical knowledge (e.g., "I know you need your dog, Buster, to come with you") in the opening sequence.
    *   *The Refusal Loop:* Procedural script for treating a "No" as an ongoing baseline check-in rather than an application closure.

**Phase C: The Vagal Transition Window**
*   **Action:** Define the first 72 hours of pod occupancy as a distinct "Vagal Transition" clinical phase.
*   **Deliverable:** Specify the exact ambient conditions (acoustic limits, pod steward proximity limits, lighting) required to facilitate the shift from dorsal-vagal shutdown to ventral-vagal engagement.

---

## 3. Gap 2: Social Network Transition Protocol (SNA Pipeline)
**Objective:** Operationalize Wasserman and Faust's Social Network Analysis into an automated Process 4 data pipeline for pod assignment.

**Phase A: The Assessment Instrument**
*   **Action:** Design the data collection tool used at the encampment level.
*   **Deliverable:** A standardized, low-friction relational mapping survey (e.g., "Who watches your gear?", "Who do you eat with?").

**Phase B: SNA Scoring Logic**
*   **Action:** Define the mathematical weights for relational ties.
*   **Elements to Specify:**
    *   *Density Scoring:* Quantifying cluster strength.
    *   *Reciprocity Verification:* Confirming bidirectional trust.
    *   *Toxicity Filtering:* Identifying parasitic or exploitative nodes to deliberately sever during transition.

**Phase C: Pod Assignment Algorithm**
*   **Action:** Write the logic gates for the Process 4 cybernetic grid.
*   **Deliverable:** A procedural workflow mapping: Encampment SNA Input -> Cluster Identification -> Pod Capacity Verification -> Group Assignment Output.
