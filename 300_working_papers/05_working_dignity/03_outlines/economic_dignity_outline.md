# Outline: Economic Dignity Infrastructure (EDI)
**Working Paper 5: The Agentive Reintegration Pathway**

## I. Introduction
* **The Progression of Stabilization**: Material Dignity Infrastructure (MDI) provides physiological baseline; Relational Dignity Infrastructure (RDI) provides ontological security.
* **The Integration Gap**: Stabilization inside the tower does not equal societal reintegration. Residents face a hostile sovereign labor market that punishes resume gaps and institutional histories.
* **Thesis Statement**: Durable exit from the stabilization pipeline requires Economic Dignity Infrastructure (EDI)—a structured mechanism converting physical and relational stability into agentive selfhood through internal cooperative enterprise, bypassing hostile external labor markets.

## II. The Return Deficit and Labor Market Exclusion
* **Neurological Barriers**: Chronic caloric insufficiency produces measurable changes in behavioral economics (e.g., impulsivity). Reintegration cannot occur until these deficits are reversed in Phase 1 stabilization.
* **The Return Deficit**: Marginalized individuals experience a "return deficit" where standard job-seeking efforts yield lower returns due to structural stigma.
* **Failure of Traditional Models**: Employment Social Enterprises (ESEs) and traditional vocational rehabilitation often return vulnerable individuals to the competitive market prematurely, leading to recidivism.

## III. Identity Capital Accumulation
* **Defining Identity Capital**: The intangible psychological currency (self-efficacy, professional narrative) required for labor market navigation (Côté, 2002).
* **The Secure Environment**: MDI towers provide the closed-loop, safe environment necessary for residents to accumulate identity capital before attempting external transitions.
* **Agentic Capacity**: Moving from passive service recipient to active economic participant requires structural scaffolding rather than merely motivational rhetoric.

## IV. The Cooperative Reintegration Mechanism
* **Worker Cooperatives vs. ESEs**: Worker cooperatives transition marginalized populations directly into ownership, neutralizing external labor market biases (Pérot, 2014).
* **Internal Micro-Economies**: MDI towers establish internal cooperative enterprises (e.g., maintenance, culinary, administrative support) functioning as closed-loop economies.
* **Individual Placement and Support (IPS)**: Integrating the "place, then train" evidence-based IPS model (Drake et al., 2012; O'Connell et al., 2021) within the cooperative structure to maximize retention.

## V. The Tenancy Bridge Guarantee (Phase 2)
* **Capital Routing Integration**: Leveraging managed care billing (CalAIM) to fund the supportive employment layer, replacing reliance on government homelessness procurement.
* **Graduation to Sovereign Housing**: Once identity capital is established and cooperative economic participation is stable, individuals trigger the Tenancy Bridge Guarantee, transitioning to independent sovereign real estate.
* **Conclusion**: EDI completes the three-part dignity stack (Material, Relational, Agentive), permanently breaking the institutional dependence cycle.
