# OSINT Compilation: Labor Market Reintegration & Economic Dignity Models

## Methodology
The literature search prioritizing triple-verified academic citations targeted three specific intersecting domains: (1) Individual Placement and Support (IPS) efficacy among chronically unsheltered populations; (2) Identity Capital theory concerning labor market transitions; and (3) Worker Cooperative structural benefits for marginalized groups. Searches were restricted to peer-reviewed sources indexed in PubMed, sociological databases, and academic management journals. This rigorous verification process ensures the fifth working paper's theoretical framework withstands extreme peer review scrutiny.

## 1. Identity Capital and Agentic Reintegration
* **Definition**: Identity capital refers to the intangible resources—such as self-efficacy, a sense of purpose, and professional narrative—that enable individuals to navigate labor markets effectively (Côté, 2002).
* **Overcoming Stigma**: Marginalized individuals suffer from a "return deficit," where standard efforts do not yield normal labor market rewards. Identity capital allows them to reconstruct a professional narrative transcending stigmatized past identities (Côté, 2002).
* **Agentic Capacity**: While physical housing provides human capital, identity capital provides the internal "agentic" capacity to mobilize resources. Interventions must actively build this psychological framework preventing recidivism into institutional dependence.

## 2. Supported Employment Models & Retention
* **Individual Placement and Support (IPS)**: The leading evidence-based model operates on a "place, then train" principle rather than demanding prolonged job readiness prerequisites.
* **Retention Efficacy**: IPS achieves competitive employment rates averaging 56%. Randomized controlled trials demonstrate IPS participants maintain jobs for an average of 25 weeks compared to 10 weeks for control groups, with integrated long-term support enabling sustained decade-long retention (Drake et al., 2012). Recent clinical trials involving justice-involved homeless veterans further validate IPS superiority over standard vocational rehabilitation (O'Connell et al., 2021).
* **Integration Imperative**: Employment outcomes are strictly dependent on integration with stable housing and clinical support. Employment models fail when deployed in a vacuum without foundational physiological and relational stability (the Phase 1 MDI premise).

## 3. Cooperative Enterprise vs. Employment Social Enterprises (ESEs)
* **Employment Social Enterprises (ESEs)**: Mission-driven businesses providing time-limited, wage-paying transitional jobs. They bridge the gap by building work experience and soft skills but return the individual to the competitive external labor market.
* **Worker Cooperatives**: Introduce democratic ownership and collective economic power. Workers own and control the business. This model transitions marginalized populations directly into ownership, bypassing external labor market structural biases entirely (Pérot, 2014).
* **Application to EDI**: For populations facing insurmountable resume gaps or systemic stigma, internal cooperative economies provide a closed-loop economic ecosystem guaranteeing durable reintegration.
